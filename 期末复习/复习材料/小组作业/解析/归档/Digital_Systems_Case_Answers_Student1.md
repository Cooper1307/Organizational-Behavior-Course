# Digital Systems Case Analysis (Student 1) / Digital Systems 案例分析 (同学 1)

## Case Content / 案例内容
**同学1：**

1. **Digital Systems Case** / **Digital Systems 案例**
Top management at Digital Systems was not happy with corporate performance in 2024. So, in January 2025 the CEO, John Anderson created a new incentive pay plan where store managers would get quarterly bonuses if they achieved their performance goals. Following the first quarter (January – March 2025) under the new bonus program, over 75 percent of all store managers qualified for the bonus. / Digital Systems 的高层管理人员对 2024 年的公司业绩并不满意。因此，在 2025 年 1 月，首席执行官 John Anderson 创建了一项新的激励工资计划，如果门店经理实现了绩效目标，他们将获得季度奖金。在新的奖金计划实施后的第一个季度（2025 年 1 月至 3 月），超过 75% 的门店经理获得了奖金资格。

---

## Question 1 Analysis / 问题 1 分析
**Do you think the store managers were motivated by the incentive plan? Explain your answer utilizing one of the theories of motivation (expectancy, equity, reinforcement).** / **你认为门店经理是否受到了激励计划的激发？请利用一种动机理论（期望理论、公平理论、强化理论）来解释你的答案。**

### 1. Background / 背景
After poor performance in 2024, Digital Systems implemented a quarterly bonus plan for store managers starting in January 2025. The initial results were very positive, with more than 75% of managers qualifying for the bonus in the first quarter. / 在 2024 年业绩不佳后，Digital Systems 从 2025 年 1 月开始为门店经理实施了季度奖金计划。初步结果非常积极，超过 75% 的经理在第一季度获得了奖金资格。

### 2. Problem Identification / 问题识别
The goal is to figure out if the managers were actually motivated by this bonus plan and use OB concepts to explain why it worked so well in the first quarter. / 现在的目标是搞清楚经理们是否真的被奖金计划激励了，并用组织行为学概念解释为什么第一季度的效果这么好。

### 3. Theory Application / 理论应用
**Expectancy Theory** (期望理论) — **Victor Vroom (1964)**. / **期望理论** (Expectancy Theory) — **Victor Vroom (1964)**。

### 4. Analysis / 分析
I believe the store managers were definitely motivated by this plan, and **Expectancy Theory** helps explain why. First, since over 75% of them hit their targets, it's clear they felt the goals were actually doable with enough effort—that's high **Expectancy**. The deal was also very straightforward; the CEO promised a bonus for hitting numbers, which built strong **Instrumentality** because managers knew exactly what to expect. Finally, most managers obviously value extra pay (positive **Valence**), so having a guaranteed reward for reachable goals gave them a real reason to work harder. Basically, the plan worked because it connected their effort directly to a reward they actually wanted. / 我认为门店经理们确实受到了这个计划的激励，用**期望理论**就能很好地解释。首先，有超过 75% 的人都达标了，这说明大家觉得只要努力，目标就是能完成的——这就是高**期望**。而且规则非常直接，CEO 明确说了达标就有奖金，这种**工具性**让经理们心里很有底，知道付出会有回报。最后，大家显然都很看重奖金（正向**效价**），所以当目标可达成且回报很稳时，大家的积极性自然就上来了。简单来说，计划成功是因为它把经理们的努力和他们想要的奖励直接挂钩了。

### 5. Recommendation / 建议
The CEO should keep the bonus rules simple and transparent to maintain that sense of "if I work, I get paid." However, they should also keep an eye on the goal difficulty; if the targets are too easy, people might stop pushing themselves, which could hurt long-term motivation. / CEO 应该保持奖金规则的简单透明，维持这种“只要干了就有钱拿”的确定感。不过也要注意目标的难度，如果目标太容易达成，大家可能会产生职业倦怠或不再追求进步，这从长远来看会削弱积极性。

### 6. Key Takeaway / 核心要点
Motivation works best when people feel their hard work will actually lead to a success that pays off with something they value. / 当人们觉得努力确实能带来成功，且成功后能换来自己看重的东西时，积极性才是最高的。

### Key Terminology (关键术语摘要) / 关键术语摘要
- **Expectancy Theory** (期望理论) [Vroom, 1964] — A theory stating that motivation depends on the individual's expectations about their ability to perform tasks and receive desired rewards. / **期望理论** [Vroom, 1964] — 一种指出动机取决于个人对完成任务并获得期望奖励能力的期望的理论。
- **Expectancy** (期望) — The belief that putting in a certain amount of effort will lead to a certain level of performance. / **期望** — 相信投入一定量的努力将导致一定水平的绩效。
- **Instrumentality** (工具性) — The belief that successful performance will be followed by a specific reward or outcome. / **工具性** — 相信成功的绩效之后将伴随着特定的奖励或结果。
- **Valence** (效价) — The degree to which an individual values a particular reward or outcome. / **效价** — 个人对特定奖励或结果的重视程度。
