### 同学1：第一季度（Q1）的动机分析

**考点分析：** 
你需要解释为什么在第一季度，经理们被成功激励了。题目要求从期望理论（Expectancy）、公平理论（Equity）或强化理论（Reinforcement）中选一个。
**最佳选择：期望理论（Expectancy Theory）**。在罗宾斯的教材中，期望理论包含三个核心关系：
1. **Expectancy (Effort-Performance)**：努力-绩效关系（我觉得我能达到目标）
2. **Instrumentality (Performance-Reward)**：绩效-奖励关系（如果我达到目标，我一定会拿到奖金）
3. **Valence (Rewards-Personal Goals)**：奖励的吸引力（我想要这个奖金）

**英文示范答案（使用 Expectancy Theory，共5句）：**

Yes, I believe the store managers were highly motivated by the new incentive plan in the first quarter, which can be clearly explained utilizing **Expectancy Theory**. According to this theory, an employee's motivation is determined by the strength of the expectation that their effort will lead to performance, and that their performance will lead to a desired reward. In the first quarter, the store managers clearly exhibited high "expectancy," believing that if they put in the necessary effort, they could successfully meet the newly established performance goals. Furthermore, they demonstrated high "instrumentality," trusting that achieving these goals would actually result in the CEO's promised quarterly bonuses. Finally, the monetary bonus likely held high "valence," meaning the managers valued the reward enough to change their behavior, which ultimately led to over 75 percent of them qualifying for the bonus.

### 同学2：第二季度（Q2）的动机分析

**考点分析：**
你需要预测Q2经理们的工作动力会发生什么变化，并解释为什么。题目要求使用期望理论（Expectancy Theory）或强化理论（Reinforcement Theory）。
*   **如果用期望理论**：因为CEO赖账了，所以“Instrumentality（绩效-奖励关系）”被彻底破坏了。经理们不再相信达到绩效就能拿到钱。此外，提高目标也降低了“Expectancy（努力-绩效关系）”。
*   **如果用强化理论**：行为是其结果的函数（Behavior is a function of its consequences）。经理们努力工作却没有得到奖励，甚至被剥夺了应得的奖金，这属于“消退（Extinction）”或“惩罚（Punishment）”，会导致这种努力的行为不再发生。

我为你准备了两个版本的答案，你可以**任选其一**提交：

**选项A：使用期望理论（Expectancy Theory）无缝衔接第一题（推荐，共6句）**

In the second quarter, the store managers' motivation will undoubtedly plummet, a reaction that can be effectively explained using **Expectancy Theory**. A critical component of this theory is "instrumentality," which represents the employees' belief that achieving the required performance level will actually result in the promised reward. By unfairly refusing to pay the earned first-quarter bonuses, the CEO completely destroyed this performance-reward relationship, reducing the managers' instrumentality to near zero. Consequently, the store managers will logically assume that even if they work hard to meet the new Q2 targets, the CEO might arbitrarily change the rules and withhold their pay again. Additionally, by significantly increasing the performance requirements for the second quarter, the CEO also lowered the "expectancy" (the effort-performance link), making managers feel that the goals are now unrealistically difficult to achieve. Therefore, they will not work hard in the second quarter because they no longer trust the organization nor believe their efforts will be fairly rewarded.

**选项B：使用强化理论（Reinforcement Theory）换个角度（共6句）**

In the second quarter, the store managers’ motivation will significantly decrease, a situation that can be perfectly explained using **Reinforcement Theory**. This theory states that behavior is a function of its consequences, meaning that behaviors followed by positive outcomes will be repeated, while those followed by negative or no outcomes will not. During the first quarter, the store managers worked hard and achieved their goals, but the CEO withheld their earned bonuses, which acts as a form of "extinction" and "punishment." Because their hard work (the desired behavior) was not met with the promised positive reinforcement, the managers learned that putting in extra effort is ultimately pointless. Furthermore, increasing the performance requirements while taking away previous rewards creates a highly demotivating work environment. As a result, the store managers will not work hard in the second quarter because the reinforcing consequence has been removed, effectively extinguishing their motivated behavior.

### 1. 期望理论 (Expectancy Theory) —— 画的饼，我到底能不能吃到？

这是在第一道题和第二道题中最推荐使用的理论。著名学者弗鲁姆提出，一个人有没有动力去做一件事，取决于他在心里问自己的**三个问题**。只有这三个问题的答案都是“Yes”，他才会有动力。

**👉 灵魂三问（理论的三大核心）：**

1.  **期望 (Expectancy) - “我努力了，能做到吗？”**
    *   *生活例子*：你想考清华，但你平时数学只考30分。你觉得“我再怎么努力也考不上”，这就是**期望值极低**，所以你干脆不学了。
    *   *对应案例*：第一季度，CEO设定的目标比较合理，75%的人都觉得“努努力能达到”（期望值高）。第二季度，CEO突然把目标拔高，经理们一看“这根本不可能完成嘛”，期望值就破灭了。
2.  **工具性 (Instrumentality) - “我做到了，老板会兑现承诺吗？”**
    *   *生活例子*：你妈说“期末考前十名，给你买个PS5”。你考到了，结果你妈说“那是骗你好好学习的，不买”。**这就是工具性被破坏了**。
    *   *对应案例*：这就是CEO犯的最大的错！经理们第一季度达标了，CEO却耍赖不给钱。到了第二季度，经理们心里想：“我信你个鬼，就算我达标了，你肯定又要找借口不发奖金。”（绩效和奖励之间的连接彻底断裂）。
3.  **效价 (Valence) - “老板给的奖励，是我想要的吗？”**
    *   *生活例子*：你平时最讨厌吃榴莲，你妈说“考了一百分，奖励你吃一整个大榴莲”。你不仅没动力，甚至想考个零分。
    *   *对应案例*：案例中的奖金（钱）对绝大多数打工人来说都是极其想要的（效价很高）。

**💡 小白总结：**
期望理论就是告诉老板：**目标要够得着，说话要算数，奖品要诱人！** 案例里的CEO说话不算数（破坏了工具性），还把目标定得遥不可及（破坏了期望），所以第二季度员工彻底“摆烂”了。

---

### 2. 强化理论 (Reinforcement Theory) —— 驯兽师法则

这是第二道题的备选理论，心理学家斯金纳提出来的。这个理论非常简单粗暴：**它不关心你心里在想什么，它只看“结果”对“行为”的影响。**

核心逻辑：**种瓜得瓜，尝到甜头就会继续干，吃到苦头或者白干，就不会再干了。**

**👉 四种常用手段：**

1.  **正强化 (Positive Reinforcement)：** 做了好事 ➡️ 给糖吃。（员工加班 ➡️ 发奖金。员工以后还会愿意加班）。
2.  **负强化 (Negative Reinforcement)：** 做了好事 ➡️ 撤销讨厌的事。（员工提前完成任务 ➡️ 老板不再天天发微信催命。员工为了不被催，以后还会提前完成）。
3.  **惩罚 (Punishment)：** 做了错事 ➡️ 挨揍。（员工迟到 ➡️ 扣钱。员工以后不敢迟到了）。
4.  **消退 (Extinction)：** 做了好事 ➡️ **什么都没发生（被无视了）**。（员工拼死拼活加班 ➡️ 老板连句“谢谢”都没说，也没发钱。员工心灰意冷，以后再也不加班了）。

**💡 小白总结与案例对应：**
案例里的CEO就是一个糟糕的“驯兽师”。经理们在第一季度拼命达标了（表现出了好行为），结果CEO不仅没给“正强化”（发奖金），反而把奖金黑掉了！这就导致了**“消退 (Extinction)”**——员工发现“努力工作=什么也得不到”，于是这种努力的行为自然就消失了。

---

### 3. 公平理论 (Equity Theory) —— 不患寡而患不均

虽然我们刚才的答案主要用了前两个，但题目里提到了这个理论，我们也顺便了解一下。

核心逻辑：**员工不仅关心自己拿了多少，更关心“自己拿的”和“别人拿的”比例对不对。**

*   员工会在心里算一笔账：**我的回报 ÷ 我的付出  对比别人的回报 ÷ 别人的付出。**
*   如果发现自己拿得少，干得多，就会觉得不公平，心里就会不平衡。为了找回平衡，员工要么要求涨工资（要求增加回报），要么**开始摸鱼（减少自己的付出）**。

**💡 小白总结与案例对应：**
在案例里，经理们付出了第一季度的努力，却没有得到约定的回报，他们觉得受到了极大的不公平对待，所以第二季度的对策就是——摸鱼，不干了。

### 1. Expectancy Theory (期望理论)
*(Developed by Victor Vroom 维克托·弗鲁姆)*

**📚 Academic Definition (学术定义):**
Expectancy theory argues that the strength of a tendency to act in a certain way depends on the strength of an expectation that the act will be followed by a given outcome and on the attractiveness of that outcome to the individual.
*(中文翻译：期望理论认为，人们采取某种行为的动力，取决于他们对“行为会导致某种结果”的期望程度，以及这个结果对他们的吸引力。)*

**🔑 Three Key Relationships (核心三要素 - 考试必背考点):**
这个理论的核心就是问自己三个问题，只有三个都是 "Yes"，员工才有动力 (Motivation)。

1.  **Expectancy (Effort-Performance link) / 期望值（努力-绩效关系）**
    *   *English:* The probability perceived by the individual that exerting a given amount of effort will lead to performance.
    *   *Chinese:* 小白解释：“我努力了，能达到目标吗？”（如果目标太高，期望值就是零）。
2.  **Instrumentality (Performance-Reward link) / 工具性（绩效-奖励关系）**
    *   *English:* The degree to which the individual believes performing at a particular level will lead to the attainment of a desired outcome.
    *   *Chinese:* 小白解释：“我达标了，老板真的会发奖金兑现承诺吗？”。
3.  **Valence (Rewards-Personal Goals link) / 效价（奖励的吸引力）**
    *   *English:* The degree to which organizational rewards satisfy an individual's personal goals or needs and the attractiveness of those potential rewards.
    *   *Chinese:* 小白解释：“老板给的奖金或奖品，是我真正想要的吗？”。

**🎯 Application in the Digital Systems Case (案例应用):**
*   **Quarter 1 (Q1):** The managers were motivated because Expectancy, Instrumentality, and Valence were all high. (第一季度动力足，因为三个要素都很高)。
*   **Quarter 2 (Q2):** The CEO destroyed **Instrumentality** by withholding the Q1 bonus (managers no longer believe performance leads to rewards). Also, increasing the goals lowered the **Expectancy** (managers think the new goals are impossible). Therefore, motivation drops to zero. (第二季度，老板赖账破坏了“工具性”，提高目标破坏了“期望值”，所以员工动力归零)。

### 2. Reinforcement Theory (强化理论)
*(Based on B.F. Skinner's behaviorism 斯金纳的行为主义)*

**📚 Academic Definition (学术定义):**
Reinforcement theory argues that **behavior is a function of its consequences**. It ignores the inner state of the individual and concentrates solely on what happens when he or she takes some action.
*(中文翻译：强化理论认为“行为是其结果的函数”。它不关注人内心的想法，只关注行为产生后会带来什么后果。)*

**🔑 Four Key Concepts (四大核心手段):**

1.  **Positive Reinforcement (正强化):** Providing a rewarding consequence to encourage desired behavior. (做好事 ➡️ 给奖励/给糖吃。比如发奖金)。
2.  **Negative Reinforcement (负强化):** Removing an unpleasant consequence to encourage desired behavior. (做好事 ➡️ 撤销讨厌的事。比如表现好就不用写繁琐的报告)。
3.  **Punishment (惩罚):** Applying an unpleasant consequence to eliminate an undesirable behavior. (做错事 ➡️ 挨罚。比如迟到扣工资)。
4.  **Extinction (消退):** Withholding reinforcement for a previously rewarded behavior, causing the behavior to gradually disappear. (做好事 ➡️ 被无视，什么都没发生。员工热情逐渐消失)。

**🎯 Application in the Digital Systems Case (案例应用):**
*   The store managers worked hard to meet the goals (desired behavior). However, the CEO did not provide the promised bonus (lack of positive reinforcement). By withholding the reward, the CEO caused **Extinction**. In Q2, the managers will stop working hard because their previous hard work produced no positive consequences.
*   *(中文解析：经理们努力工作了，但老板没给“正强化”/没发奖金。这种不给奖励的行为导致了“消退”。因为努力工作没有带来好结果，第二季度他们就不干了。)*

### 3. Equity Theory (公平理论)
*(Developed by J. Stacy Adams 亚当斯)*

**📚 Academic Definition (学术定义):**
Equity theory states that individuals compare their job **inputs** (effort, experience, education) and **outcomes** (salary levels, raises, recognition) with those of others and then respond to eliminate any inequities.
*(中文翻译：公平理论认为，员工会将自己的“投入”和“产出”与别人的进行比较。如果觉得不公平，就会采取行动来消除这种不公平感。)*

**🔑 Core Mechanism (核心机制):**
Employees evaluate the ratio of: **Outcomes / Inputs** (产出 ÷ 投入)
*(员工心里有一杆秤：我的回报/我的付出 vs. 别人的回报/别人的付出)*

*   **Under-rewarded Inequity (报酬过低的不仅平):** Feeling you put in more effort but get less reward. To restore equity, employees will either ask for a raise (increase outcomes) or **reduce their effort/slack off (decrease inputs)**.
    *(中文解释：干得多拿得少。为了找回平衡，要么要求涨薪，要么开始摸鱼降低投入。)*

**🎯 Application in the Digital Systems Case (案例应用):**
*   In Q1, the managers provided high **inputs** (hard work) but received zero **outcomes** (no bonus). This creates a strong perception of inequity. To restore equity in Q2, since they cannot control the outcomes (the CEO won't pay), they will drastically reduce their **inputs** (stop working hard).
*   *(中文解析：第一季度经理们“投入”很大，但“产出/奖金”为零，极度不公平。既然没法改变产出，第二季度他们只能大幅减少“投入”，也就是开始摆烂不干了。)*
