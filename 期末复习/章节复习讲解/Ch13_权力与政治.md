## 第一部分：理论基础 / Theory Foundations

### 一、权力vs领导 / Power vs. Leadership

In organizational behavior, power simply refers to a capacity that A has to influence the behavior of B so B acts in accordance with A's wishes. Someone can thus have power but not use it; it is a capacity or potential. The most important aspect of power is that it is a function of dependence. The greater B's dependence on A, the greater A's power in the relationship.

在组织行为学中，权力简单地指A影响B行为的能力，使B按照A的意愿行事。因此，一个人可以拥有权力但不使用它；它是一种能力或潜力。权力最重要的方面是它是依赖性的函数。B对A的依赖性越大，A在关系中的权力就越大。

Leaders and power are closely intertwined, but they differ in four ways:
1. **Goal compatibility**: Power does not require goal compatibility; leadership requires some congruence between the goals of the leader and those being led.
2. **Direction of influence**: Leadership focuses on downward influence; power takes all factors into consideration (downward, lateral, upward).
3. **Focus**: Leadership emphasizes style; power focuses on tactics for gaining compliance.
4. **Scope**: Leadership concentrates on individual influence; power acknowledges groups can use power too.

领导者和权力密切相关，但有四个方面的区别：
1. **目标兼容性**：权力不需要目标兼容；领导要求领导者和被领导者的目标有一定一致性。
2. **影响方向**：领导关注向下影响；权力考虑所有因素（向下、横向、向上）。
3. **焦点**：领导强调风格；权力关注获得服从的策略。
4. **范围**：领导集中于个人影响；权力承认群体也可以使用权力。

### 二、权力的基础 / Bases of Power

**Formal Power (正式权力)**:

1. **Coercive Power (强制权力)**: Depends on the target's fear of negative results from failing to comply. Includes dismissal, suspension, demotion, assigning unpleasant work, or withholding key information.
   依赖于目标对不遵从负面结果的恐惧。包括解雇、停职、降职、分配不愉快的工作或扣留关键信息。

2. **Reward Power (奖赏权力)**: People comply because it produces positive benefits. Financial rewards (pay, bonuses) and nonfinancial rewards (recognition, promotions, interesting assignments).
   人们遵从是因为它产生积极利益。经济奖励（薪酬、奖金）和非经济奖励（认可、晋升、有趣的工作分配）。

3. **Legitimate Power (法定权力)**: The formal authority to control and use organizational resources based on the person's structural position. Broader than coercion and reward; includes members' acceptance of hierarchical authority.
   基于个人在组织中的结构位置控制和利用组织资源的正式权威。比强制和奖赏更广泛；包括成员对层级权威的接受。

**Personal Power (个人权力)**:

4. **Expert Power (专家权力)**: Influence wielded as a result of expertise, special skills, or knowledge. As jobs become more specialized, we become dependent on experts.
   因专业知识、特殊技能或知识而产生的影响力。随着工作变得更加专业化，我们变得依赖专家。

5. **Referent Power (参照权力)**: Based on identification with a person who has desirable resources or personal traits. If I like, respect, and admire you, you can exercise power over me.
   基于对拥有理想资源或个人特质的人的认同。如果我喜欢、尊重和钦佩你，你就可以对我行使权力。

> **Key Point**: Personal sources of power (expert and referent) are most effective — positively related to satisfaction, commitment, and performance. Coercive power can be damaging.  
> **记忆要点**：个人权力来源（专家和参照）最有效——与满意度、承诺和绩效正相关。强制权力可能是有害的。

### 三、依赖性：权力的关键 / Dependence: The Key to Power

The general dependence postulate: The greater B's dependence on A, the more power A has over B. When you possess anything others require that you alone control, you gain power over them.

一般依赖性假设：B对A的依赖性越大，A对B的权力就越大。当你拥有他人需要且只有你控制的东西时，你就获得了对他人的权力。

**What Creates Dependence (什么创造依赖性)**:

1. **Importance (重要性)**: If nobody wants what you have, it won't create dependence. Degrees of importance range from survival needs to fashionable items.
   如果没人想要你拥有的东西，它不会创造依赖性。重要性的程度从生存需求到时尚物品不等。

2. **Scarcity (稀缺性)**: When the supply of labor is low relative to demand, workers can negotiate better compensation. Example: computer-engineering faculty vs. English instructors.
   当劳动力供应相对于需求较低时，工人可以协商更好的薪酬。示例：计算机工程教师vs.英语教师。

3. **Nonsubstitutability (不可替代性)**: The fewer viable substitutes for a resource, the more power a person controlling that resource has.
   资源的可行替代品越少，控制该资源的人的权力就越大。

### 四、权力策略 / Power Tactics

Nine distinct influence tactics:

九种独特的影响策略：

1. **Legitimacy (合法性)**: Relying on authority position or organizational policies/rules.
   依赖权威职位或组织政策/规则。

2. **Rational persuasion (理性说服)**: Presenting logical arguments and factual evidence. (Most effective across all organizational levels)
   提出逻辑论据和事实证据。（在所有组织层级中最有效）

3. **Inspirational appeals (鼓舞性诉求)**: Appealing to values, needs, hopes, and aspirations. (Best for downward influence)
   诉诸价值观、需求、希望和抱负。（最适合向下影响）

4. **Consultation (协商)**: Involving the target in deciding how to accomplish your plan.
   让目标参与决定如何完成你的计划。

5. **Exchange (交换)**: Rewarding the target with benefits or favors in exchange for acceding to a request.
   用利益或恩惠奖励目标以交换同意请求。

6. **Personal appeals (个人诉求)**: Asking for compliance based on friendship or loyalty. (Best for lateral influence)
   基于友谊或忠诚要求遵从。（最适合横向影响）

7. **Ingratiation (讨好)**: Using flattery, praise, or friendly behavior prior to making a request.
   在提出请求之前使用奉承、赞扬或友好行为。

8. **Pressure (压力)**: Using warnings, repeated demands, and threats. (Least effective, tends to backfire)
   使用警告、重复要求和威胁。（最无效，容易适得其反）

9. **Coalitions (联盟)**: Enlisting the aid or support of others to persuade the target.
   争取他人的帮助或支持来说服目标。

**Effective Strategies (有效策略)**:
- Begin with "softer" tactics (personal appeals, inspirational, rational persuasion, consultation).
- If these fail, move to "harder" tactics (exchange, coalitions, pressure).
- A single soft tactic is more effective than a single hard tactic.
- Combining two soft tactics or soft + rational persuasion is most effective.

**有效策略**：
- 从"软"策略开始（个人诉求、鼓舞性、理性说服、协商）。
- 如果失败，转向"硬"策略（交换、联盟、压力）。
- 单一软策略比单一硬策略更有效。
- 结合两种软策略或软+理性说服最有效。

### 五、权力对人的影响 / How Power Affects People

**Negative Effects (负面影响)**:
- Power leads people to place their own interests ahead of others' needs.
- Powerful people react negatively to threats to their competence.
- Power leads to overconfident decision making and "objectifying" others.

权力使人们将自身利益置于他人需求之前。
有权力的人对能力威胁作出负面反应。
权力导致过度自信的决策制定和"物化"他人。

**Positive Effects (正面影响)**:
- Power energizes and increases motivation to achieve goals.
- Power can enhance motivation to help others.
- Power reveals what we value — with strong moral identity, power enhances moral awareness.

权力激发并增加实现目标的动力。
权力可以增强帮助他人的动机。
权力揭示我们的价值观——具有强烈道德身份时，权力增强道德意识。

**Moderators of Negative Effects (负面影响的调节因素)**:
1. Wielder's personality (anxious personality = less corruption)
2. Organizational systems (accountability stops self-serving behavior)
3. Expressing gratitude toward powerful others
4. Those with strong moral identity use power ethically

行使者的人格（焦虑人格=较少腐败）
组织系统（问责制阻止自利行为）
向有权力的人表达感激
具有强烈道德身份的人道德使用权力

### 六、组织政治 / Organizational Politics

Political behavior consists of activities not required as part of an individual's formal role but that influence, or attempt to influence, the distribution of advantages and disadvantages within the organization.

政治行为包括不作为个人正式角色的一部分但影响或试图影响组织内优势和劣势分配的活动。

**Factors Contributing to Political Behavior (促成政治行为的因素)**:

**Individual Factors (个体因素)**:
- High self-monitors, internal locus of control, high need for power
- Machiavellian personality
- More alternate job opportunities → more likely to employ politics

高自我监控者、内控型、高权力需求
马基雅维利人格
更多替代工作机会→更可能使用政治行为

**Organizational Factors (组织因素)**:
- Declining resources, changing resource patterns, promotion opportunities
- Low trust, role ambiguity, unclear performance evaluation systems
- Win-lose reward allocation, democratic decision making, high performance pressure

资源减少、资源模式变化、晋升机会
低信任、角色模糊、不明确的绩效评估系统
零和奖励分配、民主决策、高绩效压力

**Responses to Politics (对政治的反应)**:
- For most people: decreased job satisfaction, increased anxiety/stress, higher turnover, reduced performance
- When politics and understanding are both high → performance increases (see as opportunity)
- When understanding is low → politics seen as threat → negative effect on performance

对大多数人：工作满意度降低、焦虑/压力增加、离职率更高、绩效降低
当政治和理解都高时→绩效增加（视为机会）
当理解低时→政治被视为威胁→对绩效产生负面影响

### 七、印象管理 / Impression Management (IM)

IM is the process by which individuals attempt to control the impression others form of them.

IM是个人试图控制他人对自己形成印象的过程。

**IM Techniques (IM技术)**:
- **Conformity**: Agreeing with someone's opinion to gain approval.
  遵从：同意某人的意见以获得认可。
- **Favors**: Doing something nice for someone to gain approval.
  恩惠：为某人做好事以获得认可。
- **Excuses**: Explaining a predicament to minimize severity.
  借口：解释困境以最小化严重性。
- **Apologies**: Admitting responsibility and seeking pardon.
  道歉：承认责任并寻求宽恕。
- **Self-promotion**: Highlighting best qualities and achievements.
  自我推销：突出最佳品质和成就。
- **Flattery**: Complimenting others to appear perceptive and likeable.
  奉承：赞美他人以显得敏锐和讨人喜欢。
- **Exemplification**: Doing more than needed to show dedication.
  示范：做得比需要更多以展现奉献。

**IM Effectiveness (IM有效性)**:
- In interviews: IM is powerful predictor of success; structured interviews weaken IM effects.
- In performance evaluations: Ingratiation → higher ratings; self-promotion → lower ratings (unless high political skill).

在面试中：IM是成功的强大预测因子；结构化面试削弱IM效果。
在绩效评估中：讨好→更高评级；自我推销→更低评级（除非高政治技能）。

## 第三部分：考点总结 / Key Exam Points

### 🔴 高频考点（必考）

1. **权力vs领导的四个区别 / Four Differences Between Power and Leadership**: Goal compatibility, direction of influence, focus, scope.
2. **五种权力基础 / Five Bases of Power**: Coercive, reward, legitimate, expert, referent. Personal > formal.
3. **依赖性三要素 / Three Factors Creating Dependence**: Importance, scarcity, nonsubstitutability.
4. **九种权力策略 / Nine Power Tactics**: Rational persuasion most effective; pressure least effective.
5. **印象管理技术 / IM Techniques**: Seven techniques and their effects on interviews and evaluations.

### 🟡 中频考点（常考）

1. **促成政治行为的因素 / Factors Contributing to Political Behavior**: Individual and organizational factors.
2. **权力对人的影响 / Effects of Power**: Both positive and negative effects; moderators.
3. **软硬策略对比 / Soft vs. Hard Tactics**: Soft more effective; combining soft + rational persuasion best.
4. **性骚扰 / Sexual Harassment**: Definition, prevalence, manager responsibility.
5. **政治技能 / Political Skill**: Ability to influence without detection; moderates IM effectiveness.

### 🟢 低频考点（了解）

1. **社会网络分析 / Social Network Analysis**: Sociograms, brokers, information flows.
2. **零和奖励分配 / Zero-Sum Reward Allocation**: Fixed pie approach encourages politicking.
3. **跨文化权力策略偏好 / Cultural Preferences for Power Tactics**: US = rational appeal; China = coalition tactics.
4. **防御性行为 / Defensive Behaviors**: Avoiding action, blame, or change.

### 论述题 / Essay Question

**Q8:** Discuss the five bases of power and explain why personal power is more effective than formal power.

<details>
<summary>查看答案 / Answer Template</summary>

**Standard Answer:**
1. **Five Bases**: Coercive (fear-based), Reward (benefits-based), Legitimate (position-based), Expert (knowledge-based), Referent (admiration-based).
2. **Personal vs. Formal**: Expert and referent (personal) are positively related to satisfaction, commitment, and performance. Reward and legitimate are unrelated. Coercive is damaging.
3. **Why Personal is Better**: Personal power creates genuine commitment rather than mere compliance. It builds trust and respect, while formal power may create resentment or mere obedience.
</details>
