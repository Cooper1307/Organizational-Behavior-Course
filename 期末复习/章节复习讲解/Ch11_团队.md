## 第一部分：理论基础 / Theory Foundations

### 一、群体vs团队 / Groups vs. Teams

Groups and teams are not the same thing. A work group is a group that interacts primarily to share information and make decisions to help each member perform within his or her area of responsibility. Workgroups have no need or opportunity to engage in collective work with joint effort, so the group's performance is merely the summation of each member's individual contribution. There is no positive synergy that would create an overall level of performance greater than the sum of the inputs.

群体和团队不是一回事。工作群体主要互动的目的是共享信息并做出决策，以帮助每个成员在其职责范围内表现。工作群体不需要也没有机会进行需要共同协作的集体工作，因此群体的绩效仅仅是每个成员个人贡献的总和。没有积极的协同效应来创造超过输入总和的整体绩效水平。

A work team, on the other hand, generates positive synergy through coordination. The individual efforts result in a level of performance greater than the sum of the individual inputs. Whereas we can think of a work team as a subset of a workgroup, the team is constructed to be purposeful (symbiotic) in its member interaction.

而工作团队通过协调产生积极的协同效应。个人努力产生的绩效水平大于个人输入的总和。我们可以把工作团队看作工作群体的子集，团队被构造成在成员互动中具有目的性（共生的）。

> **Key Point**: Workgroup performance = sum of individual contributions. Work team performance > sum of individual inputs (positive synergy).  
> **记忆要点**：工作群体绩效=个人贡献之和。工作团队绩效>个人输入之和（积极协同）。

Management is looking for positive synergy that will create increased performance. The extensive use of teams creates the potential for an organization to generate greater outputs with no increase in employee headcount. However, merely calling a group a team doesn't automatically improve its performance.

管理者寻求能创造绩效提升的积极协同效应。团队的广泛使用为组织创造了在不增加员工人数的情况下产生更多产出的潜力。然而，仅仅把一个群体称为团队并不能自动提高其绩效。

### 二、五种团队类型 / Five Types of Team Arrangements

**Problem-Solving Teams (5-12 employees, same department)**: Groups from the same department who meet for a few hours each week to discuss ways of improving quality, efficiency, and the work environment. These teams rarely have the authority to unilaterally implement their suggestions, but if recommendations are paired with implementation processes, significant improvements can be realized. Originally seen in manufacturing plants as quality-control teams, now expanded into medical fields.

**解决问题型团队（5-12人，同一部门）**：来自同一部门的员工群体，每周会面几小时讨论提高质量、效率和工作环境的方法。这些团队很少有权单方面实施他们的建议，但如果建议与实施过程配对，可以实现重大改进。最初在制造工厂作为质量控制团队出现，现在扩展到医疗领域。

**Self-Managed Work Teams (10-15 employees, interdependent jobs)**: Groups of employees who perform highly related or interdependent jobs and take on supervisory responsibilities. Responsibilities include planning and scheduling work, assigning tasks, making operating decisions, taking action on problems, and working with suppliers and customers. Fully self-managed teams even select their own members and evaluate each other's performance.

**自我管理工作团队（10-15人，相互依赖的工作）**：执行高度相关或相互依赖工作的员工群体，承担监督责任。责任包括计划和安排工作、分配任务、做出操作决策、处理问题、与供应商和客户合作。完全自我管理的团队甚至选择自己的成员并评估彼此的绩效。

Research on self-managed teams shows mixed results: effectiveness depends on whether team-promoting behaviors are rewarded (economic rewards dependent on teammate input improves performance). Conflict can be detrimental unless members feel psychologically safe. Individuals report higher job satisfaction but sometimes also higher absenteeism and turnover rates.

自我管理型团队的研究显示混合结果：有效性取决于团队促进行为是否得到奖励（经济奖励取决于队友投入可提高绩效）。除非成员感到心理安全，否则冲突可能是有害的。个人报告更高的工作满意度，但有时也有更高的缺勤率和离职率。

**Cross-Functional Teams (same hierarchical level, different work areas)**: Teams made up of employees from about the same hierarchical level, but from different work areas, who come together to accomplish a task. An effective means for people from diverse areas to exchange information, develop new ideas, solve problems, and coordinate complex projects.

**跨职能团队（相同层级，不同工作领域）**：由大约相同层级但来自不同工作领域的员工组成的团队，聚集在一起完成任务。是来自不同领域的人们交流信息、开发新想法、解决问题和协调复杂项目的有效方式。

Challenges: (1) Power shifts occur as different expertise is needed, creating leadership ambiguity. (2) Early stages of development are often long. (3) It takes time to build trust and teamwork among people with different experiences and perspectives. Strength: collaborative effort of individuals with diverse skills from a variety of disciplines.

挑战：（1）需要不同专长时发生权力转移，造成领导模糊。（2）早期发展阶段通常很长。（3）在不同经历和观点的人之间建立信任和团队合作需要时间。优势：来自不同学科的具有不同技能的个人的协作努力。

**Virtual Teams (geographically dispersed, technology-mediated)**: Teams that use computer technology to tie together physically dispersed members to achieve a common goal. Members collaborate online using wide area networks, corporate social media, videoconferencing, and e-mail.

**虚拟团队（地理分散，技术媒介）**：使用计算机技术将地理上分散的成员联系在一起以实现共同目标的团队。成员通过广域网、企业社交媒体、视频会议和电子邮件在线协作。

Virtual teams should be managed differently: (1) Trust must be established (one inflammatory e-mail can undermine trust). (2) Progress must be monitored closely (so the team doesn't lose sight of goals and no member "disappears"). (3) Efforts and products must be publicized throughout the organization (so the team does not become invisible). Shared leadership may enhance performance.

虚拟团队应有所不同地管理：（1）必须建立信任（一封煽动性邮件可能破坏信任）。（2）必须密切监控进展（这样团队不会忘记目标，没有成员"消失"）。（3）努力和成果必须在整个组织内宣传（这样团队不会变得不可见）。共享领导可能提高绩效。

**Multiteam Systems (team of teams)**: Collections of two or more interdependent teams that share a superordinate goal. Example: emergency medical services team → emergency room team → recovery team, all working to save lives. They are the best choice when a team has become too large to be effective, or when teams with distinct functions need to be highly coordinated.

**多团队系统（团队的团队）**：两个或更多共享超级目标的相互依赖团队的集合。示例：紧急医疗服务团队→急诊室团队→恢复团队，都为拯救生命而努力。当团队变得太大无法有效运作，或者具有不同功能的团队需要高度协调时，它们是最佳选择。

Multiteam systems perform better with "boundary spanners" whose jobs coordinate efforts with all constituents. Leadership must both facilitate coordination between teams and lead them. Teams receiving more attention and engagement from leaders felt more empowered.

多团队系统在"边界跨越者"的帮助下表现更好，他们的工作是协调与各方的努力。领导必须促进团队之间的协调并领导它们。从领导获得更多关注和参与的团队感到更有能力。

### 三、有效团队的背景因素 / Team Context: Factors for Success

**Adequate Resources**: Every work team relies on resources outside the group. Scarcity directly reduces the ability to perform effectively. Important resources include timely information, proper equipment, adequate staffing, encouragement, and administrative assistance.

**充足资源**：每个工作团队都依赖群体外部的资源。稀缺直接降低了有效执行的能力。重要资源包括及时信息、适当设备、充足人员、鼓励和行政协助。

**Leadership and Structure**: Teams need agreement on who is to do what and ensuring all members share the workload. In self-managed teams, members absorb many duties typically assumed by managers. A manager's job becomes managing outside the team. In multiteam systems, leaders delegate responsibility and play the facilitator role.

**领导和结构**：团队需要就谁做什么达成一致，并确保所有成员分担工作量。在自我管理型团队中，成员承担通常由管理者承担的许多职责。管理者的工作变成管理团队外部的事务。在多团队系统中，领导者委派责任并扮演促进者角色。

**Climate of Trust**: Trust is the foundation of leadership; it allows a team to accept and commit to the leader's goals and decisions. Interpersonal trust among team members facilitates cooperation, reduces the need to monitor each other, and bonds individuals. Trust levels that are asymmetric and imbalanced can mitigate performance advantages — coalitions form that undermine the team as a whole.

**信任氛围**：信任是领导的基础；它使团队能够接受并致力于领导者的目标和决策。团队成员之间的人际信任促进合作，减少相互监控的需要，并将个人联系在一起。不对称和不平衡的信任水平可能抵消高整体信任的绩效优势——形成破坏整个团队的联盟。

**Performance Evaluation and Reward System**: Individual evaluations may interfere with team development. Management should utilize hybrid performance systems that incorporate both individual member components (recognize individual contributions) and group rewards (recognize positive team outcomes). Group-based appraisals, profit sharing, and small-group incentives reinforce team effort.

**绩效评估和奖励系统**：个人评估可能会干扰团队发展。管理层应使用混合绩效系统，包括个人成员部分（认可个人贡献）和群体奖励（认可积极团队成果）。基于群体的评估、利润分享和小组激励强化团队努力。

### 四、团队组成 / Team Composition

**Abilities of Members**: A team's performance depends on knowledge, skills, and abilities of individual members. High-ability teams do better on complex problems, are more adaptable to changing situations, and can apply existing knowledge to new problems. The ability of the team's leader matters — smart leaders help less intelligent members; a less intelligent leader can neutralize a high-ability team's effect.

**成员能力**：团队的绩效取决于个人成员的知识、技能和能力。高能力团队在复杂问题上表现更好，更能适应变化的情况，并能将现有知识应用于新问题。团队领导者的能力很重要——聪明的领导者帮助不太聪明的成员；不太聪明的领导者可能抵消高能力团队的效果。

**Personality of Members**: Conscientiousness is especially important — conscientious people back up other team members and sense when support is needed. Teams should NOT be "seeded" with mixed conscientiousness levels; instead, form separate high and low conscientiousness teams because mixed teams force highly conscientious members to lower their expectations.

**成员人格**：尽责性尤其重要——尽责的人支持其他团队成员，并在需要时感知到支持。团队不应"混合"不同尽责性水平；相反，应分别形成高和低尽责性团队，因为混合团队迫使高尽责性成员降低期望。

Openness to experience enhances performance (especially with constructive task conflict). Emotional stability helps teams handle conflict. Agreeableness matters — teams do worse with one or more highly disagreeable members.

开放经验提高绩效（特别是在建设性任务冲突时）。情绪稳定性帮助团队处理冲突。宜人性很重要——团队在一个或多个高度不宜人成员时表现更差。

**Allocation of Roles**: Nine potential team roles exist. Put the most able, experienced, and conscientious workers in the most central roles (those who handle more workflow and are central to all work processes).

**角色分配**：存在九种潜在的团队角色。将最能干、最有经验和最尽责的工人放在最核心的角色（那些处理更多工作流程并对所有工作流程至关重要的角色）。

**Diversity of Members**: Organizational demography suggests turnover will be greater among those with dissimilar experiences because communication is more difficult and conflict is more likely. Demographic diversity is essentially unrelated to team performance (race and gender diversity may actually be negatively related). Diversity in function, education, and expertise is positively related to performance, but effects are small.

**成员多元化**：组织人口统计学表明，经历不同的人之间离职率更高，因为沟通更困难，冲突更可能。人口多元化与团队绩效基本无关（种族和性别多元化实际上可能负相关）。功能、教育和专业知识的多元化与绩效正相关，但影响很小。

**Cultural Differences**: Cultural diversity interferes with team processes in the short term but difficulties dissipate with time. Cultural diversity is an asset for tasks requiring various viewpoints.

**文化差异**：文化多样性在短期内干扰团队流程，但困难随时间消散。文化多样性是需要各种观点的任务的资产。

**Size of Teams**: The most effective teams have 5-9 members. Amazon CEO Jeff Bezos uses the "two-pizza" rule (if it takes more than two pizzas to feed the team, it's too large). When teams have excess members, cohesiveness and mutual accountability decline, social loafing increases, and people communicate less.

**团队规模**：最有效的团队有5-9人。亚马逊CEO杰夫·贝佐斯使用"两个披萨"规则（如果需要超过两个披萨来养活团队，团队就太大了）。当团队有过多的成员时，凝聚力和相互责任感下降，社会惰性增加，人们的沟通减少。

**Member Preferences**: Not every employee is a team player. When people who prefer to work alone are required to team up, there is a direct threat to morale. Managers should consider individual preferences along with abilities, personalities, and skills.

**成员偏好**：并非每个员工都是团队成员。当喜欢独自工作的人被要求组队时，对士气构成直接威胁。管理者应考虑个人偏好以及能力、人格和技能。

### 五、团队过程 / Team Processes

**Common Plan and Purpose**: Effective teams analyze the team's mission, develop goals, and create strategies. Effective teams show reflexivity — they reflect on and adjust their purpose when necessary. Teams high in reflexivity are better able to adapt to conflicting plans and goals.

**共同计划和目的**：有效团队分析团队使命、制定目标并创建策略。有效团队展现反思性——他们在必要时反思和调整目的。反思性高的团队能更好地适应冲突的计划和目标。

**Specific Goals**: Successful teams translate their common purpose into specific, measurable, and realistic performance goals. Specific goals facilitate clear communication and help teams maintain focus on results. Team goals should be challenging — difficult but achievable goals raise team performance.

**具体目标**：成功团队将共同目的转化为具体、可衡量和现实的绩效目标。具体目标促进清晰沟通并帮助团队专注于结果。团队目标应具有挑战性——困难但可实现的目标提高团队绩效。

**Team Efficacy**: Effective teams have confidence in themselves; they believe they can succeed. Teams that have been successful raise their beliefs about future success, which motivates them to work harder. Management can increase team efficacy by helping teams achieve small successes and providing training to improve technical and interpersonal skills.

**团队效能**：有效团队对自己有信心；他们相信自己能成功。成功的团队提高对未来成功的信念，这激励他们更努力地工作。管理层可以通过帮助团队取得小成功并提供培训来提高技术和人际技能来增加团队效能。

**Team Identity**: When people connect emotionally with their teams, they invest more in the relationship. By recognizing individuals' specific skills and creating a climate of respect and inclusion, leaders foster positive team identity. Organizational identity is also important — individuals with positive team identity but without positive organizational identity can become fixed to their teams and unwilling to coordinate with other teams.

**团队认同**：当人们与团队建立情感联系时，他们在关系中投入更多。通过认可个人的特定技能并创造尊重和包容的氛围，领导者培养积极的团队认同。组织认同也很重要——具有积极团队认同但没有积极组织认同的人可能变得固守自己的团队，不愿意与其他团队协调。

**Team Cohesion**: Members are emotionally attached to one another and motivated toward the team because of their attachment. If team cohesion is high and tasks are complex, investments in promotions, rewards, and training yield greater profitable team creativity. Negative relationships reduce cohesion; teams can foster interdependence and high-quality interpersonal interactions to mitigate.

**团队凝聚力**：成员彼此情感依恋，并因这种依恋而被激励为团队工作。如果团队凝聚力高且任务复杂，在推广、奖励和培训上的投资会产生更多有利可图的团队创造力。消极关系减少凝聚力；团队可以培养相互依赖和高质量人际互动来缓解。

**Mental Models**: Effective teams share accurate mental models — organized mental representations of key elements within the team's environment. If team members have wrong mental models (likely under acute stress), their performance suffers. What matters is the sequencing of communication to maintain shared mental models.

**心理模型**：有效团队共享准确的心理模型——团队环境中关键要素的有组织心理表征。如果团队成员有错误的心理模型（在急性压力下可能发生），他们的绩效受损。重要的是维护共享心理模型的沟通顺序。

**Conflict Levels**: Relationship conflicts (interpersonal incompatibility, tension, animosity) are almost always dysfunctional. Task conflicts (disagreements about task content) stimulate discussion, promote critical assessment, and can lead to better decisions in nonroutine activities. Moderate levels of task conflict during initial phases are positively related to team creativity; both very low and very high levels negatively relate to performance.

**冲突水平**：关系冲突（人际不兼容、紧张、敌意）几乎总是功能失调的。任务冲突（对任务内容的分歧）在非日常活动中激发讨论、促进批判性评估并可能导致更好的决策。初始阶段中等水平的任务冲突与团队创造力正相关；非常低和非常高的水平都与绩效负相关。

**Social Loafing**: Effective teams undermine social loafing by making members individually and jointly accountable for the team's purpose, goals, and approach. Members should be clear on what they are individually and jointly responsible for.

**社会惰性**：有效团队通过让成员对团队的目的、目标和方法承担个人和共同责任来削弱社会惰性。成员应清楚他们个人和共同负责的事项。

### 六、将个人变成团队成员 / Turning Individuals into Team Players

**Selecting (Hiring Team Players)**: Managers should ensure candidates can fulfill team roles as well as technical requirements. Personal traits make some people better candidates for diverse teams. Teams made of members who like to work through difficult mental puzzles seem more effective.

**选拔（招聘团队成员）**：管理者应确保候选人既能履行团队角色又能满足技术要求。个人特质使某些人成为多元化团队的更好候选人。由喜欢解决困难心理难题的成员组成的团队似乎更有效。

**Training (Creating Team Players)**: Workshops help employees improve problem-solving, communication, negotiation, conflict-management, and coaching skills. An effective team doesn't develop overnight — it takes time.

**培训（创造团队成员）**：研讨会帮助员工提高解决问题、沟通、谈判、冲突管理和辅导技能。一个有效的团队不是一夜之间形成的——需要时间。

**Rewarding (Providing Incentives)**: Traditional reward systems must be reworked to encourage cooperative efforts. Promotions, pay raises, and recognition should be given to individuals who work effectively as team members. Balance individual contributions with selfless contributions to the team. Don't forget intrinsic rewards like camaraderie.

**奖励（提供激励）**：传统奖励系统必须重新设计以鼓励合作努力。晋升、加薪和认可应给予作为团队成员有效工作的个人。平衡个人贡献与对团队的无私奉献。不要忘记内在奖励，如同志情谊。

### 七、何时使用团队 / When Teams Aren't Always the Answer

Teamwork takes more time and often more resources than individual work. Apply three tests:

团队合作比个人工作花费更多时间，通常也消耗更多资源。应用三个测试：

1. Can the work be done better by more than one person? (complexity, need for different perspectives)
   一个以上的人能更好地完成这项工作吗？（复杂性、需要不同观点）

2. Does the work create a common purpose or set of goals more than the aggregate of individual goals?
   工作是否创造了超过个人目标总和的共同目的或目标集？

3. Are the members interdependent? (success of whole depends on success of each; success of each depends on success of whole)
   成员相互依赖吗？（整体的成功取决于每个成员的成功；每个成员的成功取决于整体的成功）

## 第三部分：考点总结 / Key Exam Points

### 🔴 高频考点（必考）

1. **工作群体vs工作团队 / Workgroup vs. Work Team**: Performance = sum vs. > sum (positive synergy). This is the fundamental distinction.
2. **五种团队类型 / Five Types of Teams**: Problem-solving, self-managed, cross-functional, virtual, multiteam. Know the defining characteristics of each.
3. **有效团队的三要素 / Three Components of Effective Teams**: Context, composition, process. Know the factors within each.
4. **团队效能 / Team Efficacy**: Definition, how to increase it (small successes + training).
5. **团队凝聚力与绩效的关系 / Team Cohesion and Performance**: High cohesion + complex tasks = greater creativity.
6. **冲突水平 / Conflict Levels**: Relationship conflict (always bad) vs. task conflict (moderate = good for creativity).
7. **将个人变成团队成员 / Turning Individuals into Team Players**: Selecting, training, rewarding.

### 🟡 中频考点（常考）

1. **团队组成——人格 / Team Composition — Personality**: Conscientiousness is most important; don't mix levels; seed separately.
2. **团队规模 / Team Size**: 5-9 members optimal; "two-pizza" rule; what happens with excess members.
3. **心理模型 / Mental Models**: Shared accurate representations; sequencing of communication matters.
4. **反思性 / Reflexivity**: Teams reflect on and adjust purpose when necessary.
5. **跨职能团队的挑战 / Challenges of Cross-Functional Teams**: Power shifts, long development, time to build trust.
6. **虚拟团队管理 / Managing Virtual Teams**: Trust, monitoring progress, publicizing efforts.
7. **社会惰性的防止 / Preventing Social Loafing**: Individual and joint accountability.

### 🟢 低频考点（了解）

1. **组织人口统计学 / Organizational Demography**: Diversity effects on turnover.
2. **文化差异对团队的影响 / Cultural Differences in Teams**: Short-term interference, long-term asset.
3. **多团队系统的边界跨越者 / Boundary Spanners in Multiteam Systems**: Coordination role.
4. **团队认同vs组织认同 / Team Identity vs. Organizational Identity**: Both needed for coordination.
5. **三个团队测试 / Three Team Tests**: When to use teams vs. individuals.

#### 论述题 / Essay Question

**Q11:** Discuss the characteristics of effective teams. What contextual, compositional, and process factors contribute to team effectiveness?

<details>
<summary>查看答案 / Answer Template</summary>

**Standard Answer Template / 标准答案模板:**

1. **Introduction / 引言**

   Effective teams have certain common characteristics organized into three categories: resources and contextual influences, team composition, and process variables. Merely calling a group a team doesn't automatically improve performance — teams must possess these characteristics.

   有效团队具有某些共同特征，分为三类：资源和背景影响、团队组成和过程变量。仅仅把一个群体称为团队并不能自动提高绩效——团队必须具备这些特征。

2. **Contextual Factors / 背景因素**

   Four factors: (a) Adequate resources — timely information, proper equipment, staffing. (b) Leadership and structure — agreement on roles, shared workload. (c) Climate of trust — foundation of leadership, facilitates cooperation. (d) Performance evaluation and reward system — hybrid systems with individual and group components.

   四个因素：（a）充足资源——及时信息、适当设备、人员配备。（b）领导和结构——就角色达成一致、分担工作量。（c）信任氛围——领导的基础、促进合作。（d）绩效评估和奖励系统——包含个人和群体部分的混合系统。

3. **Composition Factors / 组成因素**

   Key factors: (a) Abilities — high-ability teams perform better on complex problems. (b) Personality — conscientiousness is most important; don't mix levels. (c) Roles — nine potential roles; put best workers in central roles. (d) Diversity — mixed effects; functional/educational diversity positive. (e) Size — 5-9 members optimal. (f) Preferences — select members who prefer teamwork.

   关键因素：（a）能力——高能力团队在复杂问题上表现更好。（b）人格——尽责性最重要；不要混合水平。（c）角色——九种潜在角色；把最好的工人放在核心角色。（d）多元化——混合影响；功能/教育多元化积极。（e）规模——5-9人最佳。（f）偏好——选择喜欢团队合作的成员。

4. **Process Factors / 过程因素**

   Key factors: (a) Common plan and purpose — with reflexivity. (b) Specific, measurable, challenging goals. (c) Team efficacy — confidence in success. (d) Team identity — emotional connection. (e) Team cohesion — emotional attachment. (f) Shared mental models — accurate representations. (g) Moderate task conflict, low relationship conflict. (h) Individual and joint accountability to prevent social loafing.

   关键因素：（a）共同计划和目的——具有反思性。（b）具体、可衡量、具有挑战性的目标。（c）团队效能——对成功的信心。（d）团队认同——情感联系。（e）团队凝聚力——情感依恋。（f）共享心理模型——准确表征。（g）中等任务冲突、低关系冲突。（h）个人和共同责任以防止社会惰性。

5. **Turning Individuals into Team Players / 将个人变成团队成员**

   Three approaches: (a) Selecting — hire candidates with interpersonal skills. (b) Training — workshops for problem-solving, communication, negotiation. (c) Rewarding — hybrid systems balancing individual and team contributions.

   三种方法：（a）选拔——招聘具有人际技能的候选人。（b）培训——解决问题、沟通、谈判的研讨会。（c）奖励——平衡个人和团队贡献的混合系统。

### 论述题答题模板 / Essay Answer Template

**Sample Question / 题目示例:** Compare and contrast groups and teams. When should organizations use teams?

**Standard Answer / 标准答案:**

1. **Define Both / 定义两者**

   A work group interacts primarily to share information and make decisions to help members perform within their areas of responsibility. Performance equals the sum of individual contributions. A work team generates positive synergy through coordinated effort, producing performance greater than the sum of individual inputs.

   工作群体主要互动以共享信息并做出决策，帮助成员在各自职责范围内表现。绩效等于个人贡献的总和。工作团队通过协调努力产生积极协同效应，产生超过个人输入总和的绩效。

2. **Key Differences / 关键区别**

   The key difference is synergy. Work groups have no positive synergy — output equals the sum of inputs. Work teams create positive synergy — output exceeds the sum of inputs. Both have behavioral expectations, normalization efforts, group dynamics, and decision making, but the team's effort is purposeful and symbiotic.

   关键区别是协同效应。工作群体没有积极协同效应——产出等于输入之和。工作团队创造积极协同效应——产出超过输入之和。两者都有行为期望、规范化努力、群体动力学和决策，但团队的努力是有目的和共生的。

3. **Three Tests for Using Teams / 使用团队的三个测试**

   (1) Can the work be done better by more than one person? Complexity and need for diverse perspectives indicate teams. (2) Does the work create a common purpose beyond individual goals? (3) Are members interdependent — does success of whole depend on each, and success of each depend on whole?

   （1）一个以上的人能更好地完成工作吗？复杂性和对多样化观点的需求表明需要团队。（2）工作是否创造了超越个人目标的共同目的？（3）成员相互依赖吗——整体的成功取决于每个人，每个人的成功取决于整体？

4. **When Not to Use Teams / 何时不使用团队**

   Teamwork takes more time and resources. Simple tasks not requiring diverse inputs are better left to individuals. When tasks don't benefit from interdependency, individuals are the better choice. Swim teams (except relays) are groups of individuals performing independently — not true teams.

   团队合作花费更多时间和资源。不需要多样化输入的简单任务最好留给个人。当任务不从相互依赖中受益时，个人是更好的选择。游泳队（除接力外）是独立表现的个人群体——不是真正的团队。
