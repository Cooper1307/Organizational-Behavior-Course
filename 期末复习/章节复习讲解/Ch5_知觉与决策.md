# 第五章：知觉与决策 / Chapter 5: Perception and Decision Making

## 第一部分：理论基础 / Part 1: Theoretical Foundations

### 一、知觉及其影响因素 / Perception and Its Influencing Factors

#### 1. 知觉的定义 / Definition of Perception

Perception is a process by which individuals organize and interpret their sensory impressions in order to give meaning to their environment.

知觉是个体组织和解释其感官印象，以便为其环境赋予意义的过程。

**Key Insight**: What we perceive can be substantially different from objective reality. People's behavior is based on their perception of what reality is, not on reality itself.

**关键洞察**：我们感知到的内容可能与客观现实有显著差异。人们的行为基于他们对现实的感知，而不是现实本身。

> **Memory Aid**: Perception becomes the reality from which we act.
> **记忆口诀**：感知成为我们行动所依据的现实。

---

#### 2. 影响知觉的因素 / Factors That Influence Perception

A number of factors shape and sometimes distort perception. These factors can reside in the perceiver, the object or target being perceived, or the situation in which the perception is made.

许多因素塑造（有时扭曲）知觉。这些因素可以存在于知觉者、被知觉的对象或目标、以及进行知觉的情境中。

**Three Categories of Factors / 三类因素**:

| Factor / 因素 | Description / 描述 | Example / 示例 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Perceiver / 知觉者** | Personal characteristics: attitudes, personality, motives, interests, past experiences, expectations | Supervisors perceive early-arriving employees as more conscientious |
| **Target / 目标** | Characteristics of the object being perceived; relationship to background; tendency to group similar things together | Professor in T-shirt stands out and is perceived as individualist |
| **Context/Situation / 情境** | Time, location, light, heat, or other situational factors | Same person at club vs. in Monday morning management class |

**知觉者**: 个人特征：态度、人格、动机、兴趣、过往经验、期望。主管认为早到的员工更有责任心。

**目标**: 被知觉对象的特征；与背景的关系；倾向于将相似事物归为一类。穿T恤的教授脱颖而出，被视为个人主义者。

**情境**: 时间、地点、光线、温度或其他情境因素。同一个人在周六夜店 vs. 周一早上的管理课上。

---

### 二、对他人的知觉：归因理论 / Person Perception: Attribution Theory

#### 1. 归因理论概述 / Overview of Attribution Theory

Attribution theory tries to explain the ways we judge people differently, depending on the meaning we attribute to a behavior. When we observe people, we attempt to explain their behavior. Our perception and judgment of a person's actions are influenced by the assumptions we make about that person's state of mind.

归因理论试图解释我们如何以不同方式判断他人，取决于我们对行为赋予的意义。当我们观察他人时，我们试图解释他们的行为。我们对一个人行为的知觉和判断受我们对其心理状态所做假设的影响。

**Central Question / 核心问题**: When we observe an individual's behavior, we attempt to determine whether it was internally or externally caused.

当我们观察一个人的行为时，我们试图确定它是内部原因还是外部原因造成的。

---

#### 2. 内部归因 vs. 外部归因 / Internal vs. External Causation

| Type / 类型 | Definition / 定义 | Example / 示例 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Internal Attribution / 内部归因** | Behavior is under the personal behavioral control of the individual | Employee is late because of overnight partying and oversleeping |
| **External Attribution / 外部归因** | Behavior is forced by the situation | Employee is late because of a traffic snarl |

**内部归因**: 行为受个人控制。员工迟到是因为通宵聚会和睡过头。

**外部归因**: 情境所迫。员工迟到是因为交通堵塞。

---

#### 3. 归因的三个决定因素 / Three Determining Factors in Attribution

**Distinctiveness / 区别性**: Refers to whether an individual displays different behaviors in different situations.

指个体在不同情境下是否表现出不同的行为。

> **Rule**: If behavior is unusual → external attribution. If not unusual → internal attribution.
> **规则**：如果行为不寻常→外部归因。如果寻常→内部归因。

**Consensus / 一致性**: If everyone who faces a similar situation responds in the same way, we say the behavior shows consensus.

如果每个面对相似情境的人都以相同方式回应，我们说该行为具有一致性。

> **Rule**: High consensus → external attribution. Low consensus → internal attribution.
> **规则**：高一致性→外部归因。低一致性→内部归因。

**Consistency / 一贯性**: Does the person respond the same way over time?

这个人在不同时间是否以相同方式回应？

> **Rule**: High consistency → internal attribution. Low consistency → external attribution.
> **规则**：高一贯性→内部归因。低一贯性→外部归因。

---

#### 4. 归因错误与偏差 / Errors and Biases in Attribution

**Fundamental Attribution Error / 基本归因错误**:

The tendency to underestimate the influence of external factors and overestimate the influence of internal or personal factors when making judgments about the behavior of other people.

在判断他人行为时，倾向于低估外部因素的影响，高估内部或个人因素的影响。

> **Example**: Sales manager attributes poor performance to laziness rather than competitor's innovative product.
> **示例**：销售经理将业绩差归因于懒惰，而不是竞争对手的创新产品。

**Self-Serving Bias / 自利偏差**:

The tendency to attribute our own successes to internal factors (ability or effort) while blaming failure on external factors (bad luck or difficult coworkers). We also tend to attribute ambiguous information as relatively flattering, accept positive feedback, and reject negative feedback.

倾向于将自己的成功归因于内部因素（能力或努力），而将失败归咎于外部因素（运气不好或难相处的同事）。我们也倾向于将模糊信息解读为相对有利，接受正面反馈，拒绝负面反馈。

> **Cultural Note**: Self-serving biases may be less common in East Asian cultures, but evidence suggests they still operate there.
> **文化提示**：自利偏差在东亚文化中可能较少见，但证据表明它们在那里仍然存在。

---

### 三、判断他人的常见捷径 / Common Shortcuts in Judging Others

Shortcuts for judging others often allow us to make accurate perceptions rapidly and provide valid data for making predictions. However, they can also result in significant distortions.

判断他人的捷径通常使我们能够快速做出准确的知觉，并提供有效数据来进行预测。然而，它们也可能导致显著的扭曲。

#### 1. 选择性知觉 / Selective Perception

The tendency to selectively interpret what one sees on the basis of one's interests, background, experience, and attitudes.

根据自己的兴趣、背景、经验和态度，有选择性地解释所看到的内容的倾向。

> **Key Point**: We can't observe everything, so we select according to our interests. We see what we want to see.
> **要点**：我们无法观察所有事物，因此根据兴趣进行选择。我们看到我们想看到的。

---

#### 2. 晕轮效应 / Halo Effect

The tendency to draw a general impression about an individual on the basis of a single characteristic, such as intelligence, sociability, or appearance.

基于单一特征（如智力、社交能力或外貌）对个体形成整体印象的倾向。

> **Warning for Managers**: Be careful not to draw inferences from small clues.
> **管理者警告**：小心不要从小的线索中推断。

---

#### 3. 对比效应 / Contrast Effects

Evaluation of a person's characteristics that is affected by comparisons with other people recently encountered who rank higher or lower on the same characteristics.

对一个人特征的评估受到最近遇到的在相同特征上排名更高或更低的其他人的比较的影响。

> **Classic Example**: "Never follow an act that has kids or animals in it." Audiences love children and animals so much that you'll look bad in comparison.
> **经典示例**："永远不要跟在有小动物或小孩的节目后面表演。"观众太喜欢小孩和动物了，相比之下你会显得很差。

---

#### 4. 刻板印象 / Stereotyping

Judging someone on the basis of one's perception of the group to which that person belongs.

基于对某人所属群体的知觉来判断该人。

> **Serious Impact**: Stereotypes can be deeply ingrained and powerful enough to influence life-and-death decisions.
> **严重影响**：刻板印象可能根深蒂固，强大到足以影响生死决策。

---

### 四、知觉与个体决策的联系 / The Link Between Perception and Individual Decision Making

Individuals make decisions, choices made from among two or more alternatives. The way individuals make decisions and the quality of their choices are largely influenced by their perceptions.

个体做出决策，即从两个或多个备选方案中做出的选择。个体做出决策的方式及其选择的质量在很大程度上受到其知觉的影响。

**Key Perceptual Issues in Decision Making / 决策中的关键知觉问题**:

1. **Problem awareness is perceptual**: One person's problem is another person's satisfactory state of affairs. Awareness that a problem exists is a perceptual issue.

   **问题意识是知觉性的**：一个人的问题是另一个人的满意状态。意识到问题存在是一个知觉问题。

2. **Information interpretation**: Every decision requires us to interpret and evaluate information. Which data are relevant? Our perceptions will answer that question.

   **信息解释**：每个决策都需要我们解释和评估信息。哪些数据是相关的？我们的知觉将回答这个问题。

3. **Difficulty saying no**: Research indicates we perceive saying no as uncomfortable, and after saying no we feel obligated to say yes to subsequent requests.

   **说不的困难**：研究表明我们将说不视为不舒服的，说不之后我们会觉得有义务对后续请求说是。

---

### 五、决策制定模型 / Decision-Making Models

#### 1. 理性决策模型 / Rational Decision-Making Model

**Rational**: Characterized by making consistent, value-maximizing choices within specified constraints.

**理性**：特征是在特定约束内做出一致的、价值最大化的选择。

**Six-Step Rational Model / 六步理性模型**:

| Step / 步骤 | Action / 行动 |
|:---|:---|
| 1 | Define the problem / 定义问题 |
| 2 | Identify the decision criteria / 确定决策标准 |
| 3 | Allocate weights to the criteria / 为标准分配权重 |
| 4 | Develop the alternatives / 开发备选方案 |
| 5 | Evaluate the alternatives / 评估备选方案 |
| 6 | Select the best alternative / 选择最佳备选方案 |

> **Ideal Assumption**: The rational model assumes the decision maker has complete information, is able to identify all relevant options in an unbiased manner, and chooses the option with the highest utility.
> **理想假设**：理性模型假设决策者拥有完整信息，能够无偏见地识别所有相关选项，并选择效用最高的选项。

> **Reality**: Most decisions don't follow the rational model; people are usually content to find an acceptable or reasonable solution rather than an optimal one.
> **现实**：大多数决策不遵循理性模型；人们通常满足于找到可接受的或合理的解决方案，而不是最优方案。

---

#### 2. 有限理性 / Bounded Rationality

A process of making decisions by constructing simplified models that extract the essential features from problems without capturing all their complexity.

通过构建简化模型来做出决策的过程，该模型从问题中提取基本特征，而不捕捉其所有复杂性。

**Satisficing / 满意原则**:

A tendency to seek solutions that are satisfactory and sufficient ("good enough" in our estimation). While not necessarily optimal, it's not always a bad method.

寻求令人满意且足够（"足够好"）的解决方案的倾向。虽然不一定最优，但并非总是坏方法。

> **Why satisficing?**: Gathering all information, computing weights, and calculating values across many criteria can cost time, energy, and money.
> **为什么满意？**: 收集所有信息、计算权重和跨多个标准计算价值可能耗费时间、精力和金钱。

---

#### 3. 直觉决策 / Intuitive Decision Making

An unconscious process created out of distilled experience. Intuitive decision making occurs outside conscious thought; relies on holistic associations; is fast; and is affectively charged (engages the emotions).

一个从提炼经验中创造的无意识过程。直觉决策发生在有意识思维之外；依赖整体联想；速度快；且带有情感色彩（调动情绪）。

> **Expert Advice**: "Intuition can be very useful as a way of setting up a hypothesis but is unacceptable as 'proof.' Use hunches derived from your experience to speculate, yes, but test those hunches with objective data and rational, dispassionate analysis."
> **专家建议**："直觉作为建立假设的方式可能非常有用，但作为'证据'是不可接受的。使用从经验中获得的直觉进行推测，是的，但要用客观数据和理性、冷静的分析来检验这些直觉。"

---

### 六、决策中的常见偏差和错误 / Common Biases and Errors in Decision Making

Decision makers engage in bounded rationality, but systematic biases and errors can creep into their judgments. Shortcuts can distort rationality.

决策者进行有限理性，但系统性的偏差和错误可能潜入他们的判断。捷径可能扭曲理性。

#### 1. 过度自信偏差 / Overconfidence Bias

We tend to be overconfident about our abilities and the abilities of others; also, we are usually not aware of this bias. When people say they're 90 percent confident about the range a certain number might take, their estimated ranges contain the correct answer only about 50 percent of the time.

我们倾向于对自己的能力和他人的能力过度自信；而且，我们通常没有意识到这种偏差。当人们说他们对某个数字可能落在的范围有90%的把握时，他们的估计范围只有约50%的时间包含正确答案。

> **Key Finding**: Individuals whose intellectual and interpersonal abilities are weakest are most likely to overestimate their performance.
> **关键发现**：智力和人际能力最弱的个体最可能高估自己的表现。

---

#### 2. 锚定偏差 / Anchoring Bias

A tendency to fixate on initial information and fail to adequately adjust for subsequent information. The mind gives a disproportionate amount of emphasis to the first information it receives.

倾向于固着于初始信息，未能充分调整后续信息。大脑对其接收到的第一条信息给予不成比例的重视。

> **Practical Application**: Anchors are widely used in advertising, management, politics, real estate, and law. The more precise your anchor is, the smaller the adjustment.
> **实际应用**：锚被广泛用于广告、管理、政治、房地产和法律。锚越精确，调整越小。

---

#### 3. 确认偏差 / Confirmation Bias

The tendency to seek out information that reaffirms past choices and to discount information that contradicts past judgments. We selectively gather information that confirms our preconceived views.

倾向于寻找重申过去选择的信息，并贬低与过去判断相矛盾的信息。我们有选择性地收集确认我们先入为主观点的信息。

> **Antidote**: Those who feel there is a strong need to be accurate in making a decision are less prone to confirmation bias.
> **解药**：那些觉得有强烈需要在决策中保持准确的人较不容易受确认偏差影响。

---

#### 4. 可用性偏差 / Availability Bias

The tendency for people to base their judgments on information that is readily available to them. Events that evoke emotions, are particularly vivid, or are more recent tend to be more available in our memory.

人们倾向于根据容易获得的信息来进行判断的倾向。唤起情绪的、特别生动的或更近的事件往往在我们的记忆中更容易被调用。

> **Example**: People fear flying more than driving. Because media give more attention to air accidents, we overstate the risk of flying and understate the risk of driving.
> **示例**：人们对飞行的恐惧超过开车。因为媒体更多关注航空事故，我们夸大了飞行风险，低估了开车风险。

---

#### 5. 承诺升级 / Escalation of Commitment

An increased commitment to a previous decision in spite of negative information. We stay with a decision even if there is clear evidence it's wrong.

尽管有负面信息，仍对之前的决策增加承诺。即使有明显证据表明决策是错误的，我们仍坚持该决策。

> **When does it occur?**: Most likely when individuals view themselves as responsible for the outcome. The fear of personal failure biases how we search for and evaluate information.
> **何时发生？**：当个体认为自己对结果负责时最可能发生。对个人失败的恐惧会扭曲我们搜索和评估信息的方式。

> **Balanced Approach**: Frequent evaluation of the spent costs and whether the next step is worth the anticipated costs.
> **平衡方法**：频繁评估已花费的成本，以及下一步是否值得预期成本。

---

#### 6. 随机性错误 / Randomness Error

The tendency of individuals to believe that they can predict the outcome of random events. Decision making suffers when we try to create meaning from random events, particularly when we turn imaginary patterns into superstitions.

个体相信自己能够预测随机事件结果的倾向。当我们试图从随机事件中创造意义时，特别是当我们把想象的模式变成迷信时，决策会受到影响。

> **Example**: "I never make important decisions on Friday the 13th" or wearing a lucky T-shirt.
> **示例**："我绝不在13号星期五做重要决策"或穿幸运T恤。

---

#### 7. 风险规避 / Risk Aversion

The tendency to prefer a sure gain of a moderate amount over a riskier outcome, even if the riskier outcome might have a higher expected payoff.

倾向于选择确定获得中等金额，而不是风险更高的结果，即使风险更高的结果可能有更高的预期回报。

**Business Implications / 商业启示**:

1. Companies may pay commissioned employees considerably more than those on straight salaries to offset inherent risk.
   
   公司可能给佣金制员工支付比固定薪资员工高得多的报酬，以抵消固有风险。

2. Risk-averse employees will stick with established ways rather than take chances on innovative methods.
   
   风险规避型员工会坚持既定方法，而不是冒险尝试创新方法。

3. Ambitious people with power that can be taken away (most managers) appear to be especially risk averse.
   
   拥有可能被剥夺权力的有野心的人（大多数管理者）似乎特别规避风险。

---

#### 8. 后见之明偏差 / Hindsight Bias

The tendency to believe falsely, after the outcome is known, that we would have accurately predicted it. When we have feedback on the outcome, we seem good at concluding it was obvious.

在结果已知后，错误地相信我们本可以准确预测它的倾向。当我们获得结果反馈时，我们似乎善于得出它很明显的结论。

> **Malcolm Gladwell quote**: "What is clear in hindsight is rarely clear before the fact."
> **马尔科姆·格拉德威尔名言**："事后清楚的事情在事前很少清楚。"

---

### 七、影响决策的个体差异 / Individual Differences in Decision Making

| Factor / 因素 | Impact on Decision Making / 对决策的影响 |
|:---|:---|
| **Personality / 人格** | Achievement-oriented people hate to fail → escalate commitment. Dutiful people less likely to escalate. Achievement-striving individuals more susceptible to hindsight bias. |
| **Gender / 性别** | In non-stressful situations: equal quality. In stressful situations: men become more egocentric and make riskier decisions; women become more empathetic and improve. |
| **General Mental Ability / 一般心智能力** | Smart people just as likely to fall prey to anchoring, overconfidence, escalation. Once warned about errors, more intelligent people learn more quickly to avoid them. |
| **Culture / 文化** | Differ in time orientation, value of rationality, problem-solving vs. acceptance, collective vs. individual decision making. |

**人格**：成就导向的人讨厌失败→承诺升级。尽责的人较少升级。成就 striving 个体更容易受后见之明偏差影响。

**性别**：在无压力情境下：质量相等。在压力情境下：男性更自我中心，做更冒险决策；女性更有同理心，决策改善。

**一般心智能力**：聪明人同样容易受锚定、过度自信、承诺升级影响。一旦被警告错误，更聪明的人学得更快以避免它们。

**文化**：在时间导向、理性价值、问题解决vs接受现状、集体vs个体决策方面存在差异。

---

### 八、组织约束 / Organizational Constraints

Organizations can constrain decision makers, which is both good (to help prevent biases) and bad (to circumvent rational evaluation).

组织可能约束决策者，这既有好处（帮助防止偏差）也有坏处（规避理性评估）。

| Constraint / 约束 | Description / 描述 |
|:---|:---|
| **Performance Evaluation Systems / 绩效评估系统** | Managers shape decisions to reflect the organization's evaluation criteria |
| **Reward Systems / 奖励系统** | Suggest which choices have better personal payoffs |
| **Formal Regulations / 正式规章** | Rules and policies program decisions and limit decision choices |
| **System-Imposed Time Constraints / 系统施加的时间约束** | Deadlines make it difficult to gather all information |
| **Historical Precedents / 历史先例** | Choices made today are largely a result of choices made over the years |

**绩效评估系统**：管理者塑造决策以反映组织的评估标准。

**奖励系统**：暗示哪些选择有更好的个人回报。

**正式规章**：规则和政策程序化决策，限制决策选择。

**时间约束**：截止日期使收集所有信息变得困难。

**历史先例**：今天做出的选择在很大程度上是多年来所做选择的结果。

---

### 九、决策中的伦理 / Ethics in Decision Making

#### 1. 三种伦理决策标准 / Three Ethical Decision Criteria

**Utilitarianism / 功利主义**:

Making decisions solely on the basis of their outcomes, ideally to provide the greatest good for the greatest number. Consistent with goals such as efficiency, productivity, and high profits.

仅基于结果做出决策，理想情况下为最大多数人提供最大利益。与效率、生产力和高利润等目标一致。

**Rights-Based / 基于权利**:

Making decisions consistent with fundamental liberties and privileges, as set forth in documents such as the U.S. Bill of Rights. Respecting and protecting basic rights such as privacy, free speech, and due process.

做出与基本自由和特权一致的决策，如《美国权利法案》等文件所述。尊重和保护基本权利，如隐私权、言论自由和正当程序。

**Justice / 公正**:

Imposing and enforcing rules fairly and impartially to ensure justice or an equitable distribution of benefits and costs.

公平和公正地制定和执行规则，以确保公正或利益和成本的公平分配。

---

#### 2. 行为伦理学 / Behavioral Ethics

An area of study that analyzes how people actually behave when confronted with ethical dilemmas. Our ethical behavior varies widely from one situation to the next.

一个研究领域，分析人们在面对伦理困境时实际如何行为。我们的伦理行为在不同情境之间差异很大。

> **Key Finding**: While ethical standards exist collectively in societies and organizations, and individually in the form of personal ethics, we do not always follow ethical standards promoted by our organizations, and we sometimes violate our own standards.
> **关键发现**：虽然伦理标准在社会和组织中集体存在，并以个人伦理的形式在个体中存在，但我们并不总是遵循组织提倡的伦理标准，有时甚至违反自己的标准。

### 词汇表 / Vocabulary

### 单项选择题 / Multiple Choice Questions

**Q1.** When Katelyn performs at about the same level on related tasks (low distinctiveness), other employees perform differently than her (low consensus), and her performance is consistent over time (high consistency), what attribution will likely be made?

当Katelyn在相关任务上表现水平大致相同（低区别性），其他员工表现与她不同（低一致性），且她的表现一贯如此（高一贯性）时，可能会做出什么归因？

A. External attribution / 外部归因
B. Internal attribution / 内部归因
C. No attribution / 无归因
D. Random attribution / 随机归因

<details>
<summary>✅ 答案 / Answer: B</summary>

Low distinctiveness + Low consensus + High consistency = Internal attribution. Katelyn will be held primarily responsible for her task performance.

低区别性+低一致性+高一贯性=内部归因。Katelyn将主要对她自己的任务表现负责。
</details>

---

**Q2.** A manager attributes an employee's poor performance to laziness rather than considering market conditions. This is an example of:

一位管理者将员工的差表现归因于懒惰，而不是考虑市场状况。这是以下哪项的示例？

A. Self-serving bias / 自利偏差
B. Halo effect / 晕轮效应
C. Fundamental attribution error / 基本归因错误
D. Confirmation bias / 确认偏差

<details>
<summary>✅ 答案 / Answer: C</summary>

The fundamental attribution error is underestimating external factors and overestimating internal factors when judging others.

基本归因错误是在判断他人时低估外部因素，高估内部因素。
</details>

**Q3.** Which decision-making model involves constructing simplified models that extract essential features without capturing all complexity?

哪种决策模型涉及构建简化模型，提取基本特征而不捕捉所有复杂性？

A. Rational decision-making / 理性决策

C. Intuitive decision-making / 直觉决策
D. Evidence-based management / 循证管理

<details>
<summary>✅ 答案 / Answer: B</summary>

Bounded rationality operates within the confines of our limited information-processing capability. We construct simplified models and satisfice.

有限理性在我们有限的信息处理能力范围内运作。我们构建简化模型并满意即可。
</details>

**Q4.** An investor continues to put money into a failing stock because they feel personally responsible for the initial investment. This demonstrates:

一位投资者继续向一只亏损的股票投入资金，因为他们对初始投资感到个人责任。这展示了：

<details>

Escalation of commitment refers to staying with a decision even if there is clear evidence it's wrong, especially when individuals view themselves as responsible for the outcome.

承诺升级指即使有明显证据表明决策是错误的仍坚持该决策，特别是当个体认为自己对结果负责时。
</details>

### 判断题 / True or False

**Q6.** The self-serving bias is more common in East Asian cultures than in Western cultures.

自利偏差在东亚文化中比在西方文化中更常见。

<details>
<summary>✅ 答案 / Answer: False</summary>

Self-serving biases may be less common in East Asian cultures, but evidence suggests they still operate there. It might just take more evidence for Asian managers to conclude someone else should be blamed.

自利偏差在东亚文化中可能较少见，但证据表明它们在那里仍然存在。可能只是东亚管理者需要更多证据才能得出他人应受责备的结论。
</summary>
</details>

**Q7.** Intuitive decision making is always wrong and contradicts rational analysis.

直觉决策总是错误的，并与理性分析相矛盾。

<details>
<summary>✅ 答案 / Answer: False</summary>

While intuition isn't rational, it isn't necessarily wrong. Nor does it always contradict rational analysis; the two can complement each other. Best advice: Use intuition to set up hypotheses, but test with objective data.

虽然直觉不是理性的，但它不一定错误。它也不总是与理性分析相矛盾；两者可以互补。最佳建议：使用直觉建立假设，但用客观数据检验。
</details>

Smart people are just as likely to fall prey to anchoring, overconfidence, and escalation of commitment. However, once warned about decision-making errors, more intelligent people learn more quickly to avoid them.

聪明人同样容易受锚定、过度自信和承诺升级的影响。然而，一旦被警告决策错误，更聪明的人学得更快以避免它们。

### 论述题模板 / Essay Question Template

   高共识→外部 | 低共识→内部
   高一贯性→内部 | 低一贯性→外部

**Four Strategies / 四种策略**:

   清晰目标使决策更容易，帮助消除不一致选项。

2. **Look for Disconfirming Information / 寻找反面信息**: Actively seek information that contradicts beliefs. Challenge your own assumptions.

Performance evaluation, reward systems, formal regulations, time constraints, historical precedents all shape decisions.

绩效评估、奖励系统、正式规章、时间约束、历史先例都塑造决策。

**9. 三种伦理标准指导道德决策 / Three Ethical Criteria Guide Moral Decisions**

- Utilitarianism: greatest good for greatest number
- 功利主义：最大多数人的最大利益
- Rights: protect fundamental liberties
- 权利：保护基本自由
- Justice: fair rules, equitable distribution
- 公正：公平规则、公平分配

**10. 减少偏差的四策略 / Four Strategies to Reduce Biases**

Focus on goals → Look for disconfirming information → Don't create meaning from randomness → Increase options

聚焦目标→寻找反面信息→不从随机事件中创造意义→增加选项
