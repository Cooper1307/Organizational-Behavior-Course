## 第一部分：理论基础 / Theory Foundations

### 一、变革力量与类型 / Forces for Change and Types

Change occurs along six dimensions:
1. **Changing nature of the workforce**: Multicultural environment, demographic changes, immigration, outsourcing.
2. **Technology**: Continually changing jobs and organizations; office becoming antiquated.
3. **Economic shocks**: Great recession, job losses, corporation disappearances.
4. **Competition**: Global competitors; need for flexibility and responsiveness.
5. **Social trends**: Chat rooms, blogs, "green" practices becoming expected.
6. **World politics**: Financial crises, rise of China, government shakeups.

变革发生在六个维度：
1. **劳动力性质变化**：多元文化环境、人口变化、移民、外包。
2. **技术**：不断改变工作和组织；办公室概念变得过时。
3. **经济冲击**：大衰退、失业、公司消失。
4. **竞争**：全球竞争对手；需要灵活性和响应能力。
5. **社会趋势**：聊天室、博客、"绿色"实践成为期望。
6. **世界政治**：金融危机、中国崛起、政府震荡。

**Reactionary vs. Planned Change (被动变革vs.计划变革)**:
- Change = making things different.
- Planned change = intentional, goal-oriented activities.
- Two goals of planned change: (1) improve organization's ability to adapt to environment; (2) change employee behavior.
- **Change agents**: People who act as catalysts and assume responsibility for managing change. Can be managers, non-managers, current/new employees, or outside consultants.

变革=让事情变得不同。
计划变革=有目的的、目标导向的活动。
计划变革的两个目标：（1）提高组织适应环境的能力；（2）改变员工行为。
**变革推动者**：充当催化剂并承担管理变革责任的人。可以是管理者、非管理者、现任/新员工或外部顾问。

### 二、变革阻力 / Resistance to Change

Our egos are fragile; we often see change as threatening. Resistance can come from any level — not just lower levels. Higher-level managers may resist changes proposed by subordinates, especially if focused on immediate performance.

我们的自我是脆弱的；我们常将变革视为威胁。阻力可以来自任何层级——不仅是基层。高层管理者也可能抵制下属提出的变革，特别是如果他们关注即时绩效。

Resistance can be positive if it leads to open discussion and debate — indicates engagement. Resistance doesn't surface in standardized ways: it can be overt, implicit, immediate, or deferred. Deferred actions are more difficult to recognize and cloud the link between change and reaction.

如果阻力导致公开讨论和辩论，可能是积极的——表明参与。阻力不以标准化方式出现：可以是公开的、隐性的、即时的或延迟的。延迟行动更难识别，模糊了变革与反应之间的联系。

**Individual Sources of Resistance (个体阻力来源)**:
1. **Habit (习惯)**: To cope with complexities, we rely on habits. Change disrupts programmed responses.
2. **Security (安全感)**: People with high need for security resist change because it threatens safety.
3. **Economic factors (经济因素)**: Fear of not being able to perform new tasks to previous standards, especially when pay tied to productivity.
4. **Fear of the unknown (对未知的恐惧)**: Change substitutes ambiguity and uncertainty.
5. **Selective information processing (选择性信息处理)**: Individuals selectively process information to keep perceptions intact; hear what they want to hear.

**Organizational Sources of Resistance (组织阻力来源)**:
1. **Structural inertia (结构惯性)**: Built-in mechanisms (selection processes, regulations) produce stability and counteract change.
2. **Limited focus of change (有限的变革焦点)**: Organizations consist of interdependent subsystems; limited changes tend to be nullified by the larger system.
3. **Group inertia (群体惯性)**: Even if individuals want to change, group norms may act as constraint.
4. **Threat to expertise (对专长的威胁)**: Changes may threaten specialized groups' expertise.
5. **Threat to established power relationships (对既定权力关系的威胁)**: Redistribution of decision-making authority threatens established power.

### 三、克服变革阻力的八种策略 / Eight Tactics to Overcome Resistance

1. **Communication (沟通)**: More important than ever in times of change. Balance interests of various stakeholders. Formal information sessions decrease anxiety; high-quality information increases commitment.
   变革时期比以往更重要。平衡各利益相关者的利益。正式信息会议减少焦虑；高质量信息增加承诺。

2. **Participation (参与)**: Difficult to resist a change decision in which we've participated. Involvement reduces resistance, obtains commitment, increases quality. Negatives: potential for poor solution and time consumption.
   很难抵制我们参与的变革决策。参与减少阻力、获得承诺、提高质量。缺点：可能产生差的解决方案和耗时。

3. **Building Support and Commitment (建立支持和承诺)**: When emotional commitment is low, people resist change. Counseling, new-skills training, or paid leave may facilitate adjustment when fear and anxiety are high.
   当情感承诺低时，人们抵制变革。当恐惧和焦虑高时，咨询、新技能培训或带薪休假可能促进调整。

4. **Developing Positive Relationships (发展积极关系)**: People more willing to accept changes if they trust managers implementing them. Even resistant individuals accept new ideas when supported by coworkers.
   如果人们信任实施变革的管理者，他们更愿意接受变革。即使是抵触的人在同僚支持下也会接受新想法。

5. **Implementing Changes Fairly (公平实施变革)**: Procedural fairness especially important when employees perceive outcome as negative. Employees must see reason for change and perceive implementation as consistent and fair.
   当员工认为结果为负面时，程序公平尤为重要。员工必须看到变革的原因并认为实施一致且公平。

6. **Manipulation and Cooptation (操纵和拉拢)**: Manipulation = covert influence (twisting facts, withholding information, false rumors). Cooptation = combining manipulation and participation (buy off resistance leaders). Inexpensive but can backfire if targets become aware.
   操纵=隐蔽影响（扭曲事实、扣留信息、虚假谣言）。拉拢=结合操纵和参与（收买阻力领导者）。成本低但如果目标发现可能适得其反。

7. **Selecting People Who Accept Change (选择接受变革的人)**: Open to experience, willing to take risks, flexible behavior. Positive self-concept and high risk tolerance cope better. Openness, internal locus of control, general mental ability associated with positive attitudes toward change.
   开放经验、愿意冒险、行为灵活。积极自我概念和高风险容忍度应对更好。开放性、内控型、一般心智能力与其对变革的积极态度相关。

8. **Coercion (强制)**: Direct threats or force on dissenters. Threatening with forced transfers, blocked promotions, negative evaluations. Effective when some force enacted on at least some resisters. Same drawbacks as manipulation.
   对异议者的直接威胁或武力。以强制调动、阻止晋升、负面评估威胁。当至少对一些抵抗者实施武力时有效。与操纵相同的缺点。

### 四、变革管理方法 / Approaches to Managing Change

**Lewin's Three-Step Model (卢因三步骤模型)**:
1. **Unfreezing (解冻)**: Overcome pressures of individual resistance and group conformity. Done by: (a) increasing driving forces, (b) decreasing restraining forces, or (c) combining both.
   克服个体阻力和群体从众的压力。通过：（a）增加驱动力，（b）减少约束力，或（c）结合两者。
2. **Movement (移动)**: Movement to a desired end state. Important to keep momentum going; get to and move through movement stage quickly.
   移动到期望的最终状态。保持动力很重要；快速进入并完成移动阶段。
3. **Refreezing (再冻结)**: New change must be refrozen to make it permanent. Stabilize new situation by balancing driving and restraining forces. Without this, change will be short-lived.
   新变革必须再冻结以保持永久性。通过平衡驱动力和约束力来稳定新情况。没有这一步，变革将是短暂的。

**Kotter's Eight-Step Plan (科特八步计划)**:
1. Establish sense of urgency
2. Form coalition with enough power
3. Create new vision and strategies
4. Communicate vision throughout organization
5. Empower others to act (remove barriers, encourage risk taking)
6. Plan for and reward short-term "wins"
7. Consolidate improvements, reassess, adjust
8. Reinforce changes by demonstrating relationship to success

Steps 1-4 = Lewin's "unfreezing"; Steps 5-7 = "movement"; Step 8 = "refreezing."

**Action Research (行动研究)**:
Five steps (parallels scientific method):
1. **Diagnosis (诊断)**: Identify problems
2. **Analysis (分析)**: Systematic data collection
3. **Feedback (反馈)**: Engage employees
4. **Action (行动)**: Select change action based on data
5. **Evaluation (评估)**: Assess effectiveness

Benefits: problem-focused; reduces resistance because employees are engaged.

**Organizational Development (OD) (组织发展)**:
Collection of planned change interventions built on humanistic-democratic values to improve effectiveness and well-being. Six techniques:

1. **Sensitivity Training (敏感性训练)**: Unstructured group interaction to change behavior. Descendants: diversity training, executive coaching, team building.
   无结构群体互动以改变行为。后代：多元化培训、高管辅导、团队建设。

2. **Survey Feedback (调查反馈)**: Use questionnaires to identify discrepancies among member perceptions; discussion follows.
   使用问卷识别成员感知的差异；然后讨论。

3. **Process Consultation (PC) (过程咨询)**: Outside consultant assists client to perceive, understand, and act upon process events. More task-directed than sensitivity training; client develops skills to analyze processes long after consultant leaves.
   外部顾问帮助客户感知、理解和应对过程事件。比敏感性训练更注重任务；客户在顾问离开后长期发展分析过程的技能。

4. **Team Building (团队建设)**: High-interaction activities to increase trust and openness, improve coordination, increase performance. Includes goal-setting, interpersonal relations, role analysis, team process analysis.
   高互动活动以增加信任和开放性、改善协调、提高绩效。包括目标设定、人际关系、角色分析、团队过程分析。

5. **Intergroup Development (群体间发展)**: Change groups' attitudes, stereotypes, and perceptions about each other. Each group independently lists perceptions, then shares, discusses similarities/differences, identifies causes of discrepancies, and develops solutions.
   改变群体对他人的态度、刻板印象和感知。每个群体独立列出感知，然后分享、讨论相似性/差异、识别差异原因并制定解决方案。

6. **Appreciative Inquiry (AI) (欣赏式探询)**: Focuses on organization's successes rather than problems. Four steps:
   - **Discovery (发现)**: Identify organization's strengths
   - **Dreaming (梦想)**: Speculate on possible futures
   - **Design (设计)**: Find common vision of future
   - **Destiny (命运)**: Define how to fulfill dream; write action plans

### 五、创造变革文化 / Creating a Culture for Change

**Managing Paradox (管理悖论)**: No final optimal status exists for an organization. Constant process of finding balancing point among shifting priorities. Sometimes acknowledge past success; sometimes looking backward hinders progress. Managers who think holistically and balance paradoxical factors are more effective.

组织不存在最终最优状态。在不断变化的优先事项中寻找平衡点的持续过程。有时承认过去的成功；有时回顾过去阻碍进步。整体思考并平衡悖论因素的管理者更有效。

**Stimulating Innovation Culture (刺激创新文化)**:
Innovation = applying new idea to initiating or improving a product, process, or service.

创新=应用新想法来启动或改进产品、过程或服务。

**Structural Variables (结构变量)**:
1. Organic structures (facilitate flexibility and adaptation)
2. Long tenure in management (provides legitimacy and knowledge)
3. Slack resources (afford to purchase/develop innovations, absorb failures)
4. High interunit communication (committees, task forces, cross-functional teams)

**Context and Innovation (情境与创新)**:
Encourage experimentation, reward both successes and failures, promote training and development, offer high job security, encourage idea champions.

鼓励实验、奖励成功和失败、促进培训和发展、提供高工作保障、鼓励创新倡导者。

**Idea Champions (创新倡导者)**:
Individuals who actively and enthusiastically promote new ideas, build support, overcome resistance, ensure implementation. Characteristics: extremely high self-confidence, persistence, energy, tendency to take risks, transformational leadership traits. Cultural differences: collectivists prefer cross-functional appeals; high power-distance prefer working with authority; high uncertainty avoidance prefer working within rules.

积极热情地推广新想法、建立支持、克服阻力、确保实施的个人。特征：极高自信、坚持、精力、冒险倾向、变革型领导特质。文化差异：集体主义者偏好跨职能诉求；高权力距离偏好与权威合作；高不确定性规避偏好在规则内工作。

**Learning Organization (学习型组织)**:
Five characteristics:
1. Shared vision everyone agrees on
2. People discard old ways of thinking
3. Members think of processes as system of interrelationships
4. Open communication without fear
5. People sublimate personal interests for shared vision

**管理学习型组织 / Managing Learning**:
1. Establish strategy for change and innovation
2. Redesign structure (flatten, eliminate departments, cross-functional teams)
3. Reshape culture (reward risk taking and admitting failures)

### 六、工作压力 / Stress at Work

**What Is Stress? (什么是压力？)**:
Stress is a dynamic condition in which an individual is confronted with an opportunity, demand, or resource related to what the individual desires, for which the outcome is perceived to be both uncertain and important. Stress has both positive (challenge) and negative (harmful) purposes.

压力是一种动态状态，个人面临与期望相关的机会、需求或资源，结果被认为既不确定又重要。压力既有积极（挑战）又有消极（有害）目的。

**Challenge vs. Hindrance Stressors (挑战vs.阻碍压力源)**:
- **Challenge stressors**: Associated with workload, pressure to complete tasks, time urgency. Produce less strain; in supportive environment, improve performance.
  与工作量、完成任务压力、时间紧迫相关。产生较少紧张；在支持性环境中，提高绩效。
- **Hindrance stressors**: Keep you from reaching goals (red tape, office politics, confusion over responsibilities). Reduce performance in all environments.
  阻止你实现目标（繁文缛节、办公室政治、职责混淆）。在所有环境中降低绩效。

**Demands and Resources Model (需求-资源模型)**:
- **Demands**: Responsibilities, pressures, obligations, uncertainties
- **Resources**: Things within individual's control to resolve demands
- Social support is the most important resource

需求：责任、压力、义务、不确定性
资源：个人控制内解决需求的事物
社会支持是最重要的资源

**Allostasis (动态平衡)**:
No single ideal state exists. Through allostasis, we work to find stability by changing behaviors and attitudes. Depends on allostatic load — cumulative effect of stressors given available resources.

不存在单一理想状态。通过动态平衡，我们通过改变行为和态度来寻找稳定。取决于动态负荷——给定可用资源的压力源的累积效应。

**Potential Sources of Stress (潜在压力来源)**:

1. **Environmental Factors (环境因素)**: Economic, political, technological uncertainty. Biggest reason people have trouble coping with change.
   经济、政治、技术不确定性。人们应对变革困难的最大原因。

2. **Organizational Factors (组织因素)**:
   - **Task demands**: Job design, working conditions, work layout. Most consistent factor: amount of work + looming deadlines.
     工作设计、工作条件、工作布局。最一致因素：工作量+迫在眉睫的截止日期。
   - **Role demands**: Role conflicts, role overload, role ambiguity.
     角色冲突、角色超载、角色模糊。
   - **Interpersonal demands**: Fights, bullying, incivility, harassment. Can trigger cortisol release.
     打架、霸凌、无礼、骚扰。可能引发皮质醇释放。

3. **Personal Factors (个人因素)**: Family issues, work-life conflict, personal economic problems.

**Individual Differences in Stress (压力的个体差异)**:

1. **Perception**: Stress lies in interpretation, not objective conditions.
   压力在于解释，而非客观条件。
2. **Job Experience**: Negatively related to stress (selective withdrawal + coping mechanisms develop over time).
   与压力负相关（选择性离职+应对机制随时间发展）。
3. **Social Support**: Collegial relationships buffer stress impact — best-documented relationship.
   同事关系缓冲压力影响——最有据可查的关系。
4. **Personality Traits**: Neuroticism most studied — more prone to psychological strain, find more stressors, select less adaptive coping (avoidance).
   神经质研究最多——更容易出现心理紧张、发现更多压力源、选择适应性较差的应对（回避）。

**Consequences of Stress (压力后果)**:

| 类型 / Type | 症状 / Symptoms |
|-----------|---------------|
| Physiological 生理 | Metabolism changes, increased heart/breathing rates, blood pressure, headaches, heart attacks, coronary heart disease |
| Psychological 心理 | Job dissatisfaction, tension, anxiety, irritability, boredom, procrastination, lower emotional well-being |
| Behavioral 行为 | Reduced productivity, increased absences/turnover, changes in eating habits, increased smoking/alcohol, rapid speech, sleep disorders |

**Managing Stress (管理压力)**:

**Individual Approaches (个体方法)**:
1. **Time-management techniques**: Focus on immediate goals, boost motivation
2. **Physical exercise**: Aerobics, walking, jogging, swimming — decrease detrimental physiological responses
3. **Relaxation techniques**: Meditation, hypnosis, deep breathing — 15-20 minutes/day releases strain
4. **Social support networks**: Friends, family, work colleagues provide outlet

**Organizational Approaches (组织方法)**:
1. **Selection and placement**: Experienced individuals with internal locus adapt better to high-stress jobs
2. **Goal-setting**: Specific and challenging goals + feedback reduce role ambiguity and stress
3. **Redesigning jobs**: More responsibility, meaningful work, autonomy, feedback reduce stress (but not all employees want enriched jobs)
4. **Employee involvement**: Voice in decisions increases control and reduces role stress
5. **Organizational communication**: Reduces uncertainty by lessening role ambiguity and conflict
6. **Employee sabbaticals**: Extended voluntary leaves revive workers headed for burnout
7. **Wellness programs**: Workshops for quitting smoking, controlling alcohol, weight loss, exercise; reframe stressful situations; reduce turnover

## 第三部分：考点总结 / Key Exam Points

### 🔴 高频考点（必考）

1. **卢因三步骤模型 / Lewin's Three-Step Model**: Unfreezing (3 ways), movement, refreezing. Fundamental change model.
2. **科特八步计划 / Kotter's Eight-Step Plan**: Sequential steps and correspondence to Lewin's model.
3. **变革阻力来源 / Sources of Resistance to Change**: Individual (habit, security, economic, fear of unknown, selective processing) vs. organizational (structural inertia, limited focus, group inertia, threat to expertise, threat to power).
4. **变革推动者 / Change Agents**: Definition, who can be change agents.
5. **挑战vs.阻碍压力源 / Challenge vs. Hindrance Stressors**: Definitions and effects on performance.

### 🟡 中频考点（常考）

1. **OD六种技术 / Six OD Techniques**: Sensitivity training, survey feedback, process consultation, team building, intergroup development, appreciative inquiry.
2. **欣赏式探询四步骤 / Appreciative Inquiry Four Steps**: Discovery, dreaming, design, destiny.
3. **压力后果三类别 / Three Categories of Stress Consequences**: Physiological, psychological, behavioral.
4. **需求-资源模型 / Demands-Resources Model**: Demands, resources, social support importance.
5. **克服阻力的八种策略 / Eight Tactics to Overcome Resistance**: Ranking from most to least effective.
6. **创新倡导者特征 / Idea Champions Characteristics**: Self-confidence, persistence, energy, risk taking.
7. **学习型组织五特征 / Five Characteristics of Learning Organization**: Shared vision, discard old thinking, system thinking, open communication, sublimate interests.

### 🟢 低频考点（了解）

1. **变革的六种力量 / Six Forces for Change**: Workforce, technology, economic, competition, social trends, world politics.
2. **动态平衡 / Allostasis**: No ideal state; cumulative effect of stressors.
3. **压力的个体差异调节因素 / Individual Difference Moderators**: Perception, job experience, social support, personality (neuroticism).
4. **管理学习型组织的建议 / Managing Learning Organization Suggestions**: Strategy, redesign structure, reshape culture.

### 论述题 / Essay Question

**Q8:** Discuss the sources of resistance to change and evaluate the eight tactics for overcoming resistance.

<details><summary>查看答案 / Answer Template</summary>

**Standard Answer:**
1. **Individual Sources**: Habit (programmed responses), security (threatens safety), economic factors (fear of lowered productivity), fear of unknown (ambiguity), selective processing (keep perceptions intact).
2. **Organizational Sources**: Structural inertia (built-in stability mechanisms), limited focus (interdependent subsystems), group inertia (norms as constraints), threat to expertise, threat to established power relationships.
3. **Most Effective Tactics**: Communication, participation, building support, positive relationships, fair implementation. These build trust and genuine commitment.
4. **Least Effective**: Manipulation, cooptation, coercion. These are covert and can backfire when discovered, destroying credibility.
5. **Best Practice**: Begin with softer tactics (communication, participation, support); move to harder tactics only if necessary. Select people predisposed to accept change as a proactive strategy.
