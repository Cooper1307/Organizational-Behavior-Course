## 第一部分：理论基础 / Theory Foundations

### 一、群体与群体身份 / Groups and Group Identity

In organizational behavior, a group consists of two or more individuals, interacting and interdependent, who have come together to achieve particular objectives. Groups can be either formal or informal. A formal group is defined by the organization's structure, with designated work assignments and established tasks. An informal group is neither formally structured nor organizationally determined — it meets the need for social contact.

在组织行为学中，群体由两个或更多互动且相互依赖的个人组成，他们为实现特定目标而聚集在一起。群体可以是正式的或非正式的。正式群体由组织结构定义，有指定的工作任务和既定任务。非正式群体既没有正式结构也不是组织决定的——它满足社交需求。

> **Key Point**: Formal groups are organizationally defined; informal groups emerge spontaneously for social contact.  
> **记忆要点**：正式群体由组织定义；非正式群体因社交需求自发形成。

### 二、社会认同理论 / Social Identity Theory

People often feel strongly about their groups because shared experiences amplify our perception of events. Positive shared experiences enhance our bond with groups, and research shows that sharing painful experiences also increases felt bond and trust. Our tendency to personally invest in the accomplishments of a group is the territory of social identity theory. People develop many group identities throughout their lives (organization, city, profession, religion, ethnicity, gender).

人们常常对群体有强烈感受，因为共享经历放大了我们对事件的感知。积极的共享经历增强了我们与群体的纽带，研究表明共享痛苦经历也能增强感受到的纽带和信任。我们倾向于个人投入群体成就的倾向是社会认同理论的领域。人们在生命中发展出多种群体身份（组织、城市、职业、宗教、种族、性别）。

**Ingroup Favoritism** occurs when we see members of our group as better than other people and people not in our group as all the same. People with low openness and/or low agreeableness are more susceptible to ingroup favoritism.

**内群体偏袒**发生在我们把群体成员视为比其他人更优秀，而把非群体成员视为都一样的时候。开放性低和/或宜人性低的人更容易受到内群体偏袒的影响。

**Outgroup** is the inverse of an ingroup. Whenever there is an ingroup, there is by necessity an outgroup. When there are ingroups and outgroups, there is often animosity between them, particularly regarding practices like religion.

**外群体**是内群体的反面。只要有内群体，就必然有外群体。当存在内群体和外群体时，它们之间常常存在敌意，特别是在宗教等实践方面。

### 三、群体发展阶段：间断-平衡模型 / Stages of Group Development: Punctuated-Equilibrium Model

Temporary groups with finite deadlines pass through a unique sequencing of actions:

有明确截止日期的临时群体会经历独特的行动序列：

1. **First Meeting**: The group's direction is established, sometimes in the first few seconds of the group's existence.
   **第一次会议**：群体的方向被确立，有时在群体存在的最初几秒内。

2. **Phase 1 (Inertia)**: A period of inertia — the group tends to stand still or become locked into a fixed course of action, even when gaining new insights that challenge initial patterns.
   **第一阶段（惯性期）**：一段惯性期——群体倾向于停滞或锁定在固定的行动路线上，即使获得挑战初始模式的新见解。

3. **Transition (Midpoint)**: A transition takes place exactly when the group has used up half its allotted time. The midpoint works like an alarm clock, heightening awareness that time is limited.
   **转折点（中点）**：转折恰好在群体用完一半分配时间时发生。中点像闹钟一样，增强时间有限的意识。

4. **Major Changes**: The transition initiates major changes — dropping old patterns and adopting new perspectives.
   **重大变化**：转折引发重大变化——抛弃旧模式，采用新视角。

5. **Phase 2 (Inertia)**: A second period of inertia in which the group executes plans created during the transition period.
   **第二阶段（惯性期）**：第二段惯性期，群体执行转折期间制定的计划。

6. **Final Meeting**: Characterized by markedly accelerated activity to finish its work.
   **最后一次会议**：以明显加速的活动完成工作为特征。

> **Key Point**: The punctuated-equilibrium model characterizes groups as exhibiting long periods of inertia interspersed with brief revolutionary changes triggered primarily by awareness of time and deadlines. This model applies to temporary task groups with time deadlines.  
> **记忆要点**：间断-平衡模型将群体描述为长期惯性期间穿插短暂的革命性变化，主要由时间和截止日期意识触发。该模型适用于有截止日期的临时任务群体。

### 四、群体属性1：角色 / Group Property 1: Roles

A role is a set of expected behavior patterns attributed to someone occupying a given position in a social unit. We are required to play a number of diverse roles, both on and off our jobs.

角色是归因于在社会单位中占据特定位置的人的一套期望行为模式。我们在工作和生活中都被要求扮演多种角色。

**Role Perception**: Our view of how we're supposed to act in a given situation. We get role perceptions from stimuli all around us — friends, books, films, television, and apprenticeship programs.

**角色知觉**：我们对在特定情境中应该如何行为的看法。我们从周围的刺激中获得角色知觉——朋友、书籍、电影、电视和学徒计划。

**Role Expectations**: The way others believe you should act in a given context. In the workplace, we look at role expectations through the psychological contract — an unwritten agreement between employees and employers setting out mutual expectations. Management is expected to treat employees justly, provide acceptable working conditions, and communicate clearly. Employees are expected to demonstrate good attitude, follow directions, and show loyalty.

**角色期望**：他人在特定情境中认为你应该行为的方式。在职场中，我们通过心理契约来看待角色期望——员工和雇主之间设定相互期望的非正式协议。管理层被期望公平对待员工，提供可接受的工作条件，并清晰沟通。员工被期望展现良好态度，遵循指示，并表现出忠诚度。

> **Key Point**: Violations of the psychological contract lead to greater intentions to quit, lower productivity, higher theft, and greater work withdrawal.  
> **记忆要点**：违反心理契约会导致更高的离职意向、更低的生产力、更高的偷盗率和更大的工作退缩。

**Role Conflict**: When compliance with one role requirement makes it difficult to comply with another. **Interrole conflict** occurs when the expectations of different, separate groups are in opposition (e.g., work-family conflict). Work-family conflict is one of the most significant sources of stress for most employees.

**角色冲突**：当遵守一个角色要求使遵守另一个变得困难时发生。**角色间冲突**发生在不同群体的期望相互对立时（例如，工作-家庭冲突）。工作-家庭冲突是大多数员工最重要的压力来源之一。

### 五、群体属性2：规范 / Group Property 2: Norms

Norms are acceptable standards of behavior within a group that are shared by the group's members. All groups have established norms — what members ought and ought not to do under certain circumstances.

规范是群体内被群体成员共享的可接受行为标准。所有群体都建立了规范——成员在特定情境下应该和不应该做的事情。

**Norms and Emotions**: In task groups, individuals' emotions influenced the group's emotions and vice versa. Norms dictated the experience of emotions for individuals and groups — people grew to interpret their shared emotions in the same way.

**规范与情绪**：在任务群体中，个体的情绪影响群体情绪，反之亦然。规范决定了个体和群体的情绪体验——人们逐渐以相同方式解释他们的共享情绪。

**Norms and Conformity**: Groups can place strong pressures on individual members to change their attitudes and behaviors to match the group's standard. In Asch's experiments, 75% of subjects gave at least one answer that conformed (they knew was wrong but consistent with the group). People conform most to their **reference groups** — important groups in which a person feels members are significant to him or her.

**规范与从众**：群体可以对个体成员施加强大压力，要求他们改变态度和行为以匹配群体标准。在阿希实验中，75%的被试至少给出一个从众答案（他们知道是错的但与群体一致）。人们最从众于他们的**参照群体**——一个人认为成员对他/她很重要的群体。

**Hawthorne Studies Findings**: The Hawthorne experiments (1924-1932) revealed that group dynamics, not the physical environment, influenced behavior. Workers in the bank wiring observation room established their own norm for fair output — not too much, not too little. The group enforced norms through name-calling, ridicule, and even punches. "Don't be a rate-buster" (too much) and "Don't be a chiseler" (too little).

**霍桑实验发现**：霍桑实验（1924-1932）揭示了群体动力学而非物理环境影响行为。银行配线观察室的工人建立了自己公平产量的规范——既不太少也不太多。群体通过辱骂、嘲笑甚至拳击来执行规范。"不要当产出破坏者"（太多）和"不要当偷懒者"（太少）。

**Positive Norms**: Strong positive norms (like PC norms) can increase group creativity by reducing uncertainty about group expectations. Positive group norms may beget positive outcomes if other factors are present (e.g., high group extraversion).

**积极规范**：强烈的积极规范（如政治正确规范）可以通过减少对群体期望的不确定性来提高群体创造力。如果存在其他因素（如高群体外向性），积极群体规范可能带来积极结果。

**Negative Norms — Deviant Workplace Behavior**: Counterproductive work behavior (CWB) or deviant workplace behavior is voluntary behavior that violates significant organizational norms and threatens the well-being of the organization or its members. Four categories: Production (leaving early, wasting resources), Property (sabotage, stealing), Political (showing favoritism, gossiping), Personal aggression (sexual harassment, verbal abuse). Workplace incivility increases intentions to leave, psychological stress, and physical illness.

**消极规范——越轨工作行为**：反生产工作行为（CWB）或越轨工作行为是违反重要组织规范并威胁组织或其成员福祉的自愿行为。四类：生产类（早退、浪费资源）、财产类（破坏、偷盗）、政治类（偏袒、八卦）、人身攻击类（性骚扰、言语辱骂）。职场无礼行为增加离职意向、心理压力和身体疾病。

### 六、群体属性3：地位 / Group Property 3: Status

Status is a socially defined position or rank given to groups or group members by others. Status permeates every society and is a significant motivator.

地位是由他人赋予群体或群体成员的社会定义的位置或等级。地位渗透到每个社会，是一个重要的激励因素。

**What Determines Status** (Status Characteristics Theory):
1. The power a person wields over others (controlling group outcomes)
2. A person's ability to contribute to the group's goals (critical contributions)
3. An individual's personal characteristics (good looks, intelligence, money, friendly personality)

**什么决定地位**（地位特征理论）：
1. 个人对他人拥有的权力（控制群体结果）
2. 个人对群体目标的贡献能力（关键贡献）
3. 个人的特征（美貌、智力、金钱、友好性格）

**Status and Norms**: High-status individuals may deviate from norms when they have low identification with the group. They are better able to resist conformity pressures than lower-status peers.

**地位与规范**：高地位个体在对群体认同低时可能偏离规范。他们比低地位同行更能抵抗从众压力。

**Status and Group Interaction**: People seeking higher status become more assertive — speak out more, criticize more, state commands, interrupt others. Lower-status members participate less actively. Adding some high-status individuals to a group of mid-status individuals may be advantageous, but too many high-status people harms performance.

**地位与群体互动**：寻求更高地位的人变得更加自信——更多地发言、批评、下达命令、打断他人。低地位成员参与不那么积极。向中等地位群体中添加一些高地位个体可能有利，但太多高地位者会损害绩效。

**Status Inequity**: Perceived inequity creates disequilibrium and inspires corrective behaviors. Large differences in status within groups are associated with poorer individual performance, lower health, and higher intentions for lower-status members to leave.

**地位不平等**：感知到的不平等产生失衡并激发纠正行为。群体内地位的巨大差异与较差的个人绩效、较低的健康状况和较低地位成员更高的离职意向相关。

### 七、群体属性4：规模 / Group Property 4: Size

Groups with a dozen or more members are good for gaining diverse input (fact-finding or idea-generating). Smaller groups of about seven members are better at doing something productive.

十二人或更多的群体适合获得多样化的投入（事实调查或想法生成）。约七人的较小群体更适合做有生产力的事情。

**Social Loafing**: The tendency for individuals to expend less effort when working collectively than when working individually. It directly challenges the assumption that group productivity should at least equal the sum of individual productivity. Social loafing has a Western bias — consistent with individualistic cultures but not with collectivistic societies.

**社会惰性**：个人在集体工作时比单独工作时付出更少努力的倾向。它直接挑战了群体生产力至少应等于个人生产力之和的假设。社会惰性有西方偏见——与个人主义文化一致，但与集体主义社会不一致。

**Ways to Prevent Social Loafing**:
1. Set group goals
2. Increase intergroup competition
3. Engage in peer evaluations
4. Select members with high motivation who prefer group work
5. Base group rewards partly on each member's unique contributions
6. Publicly post individual performance ratings

**防止社会惰性的方法**：
1. 设定群体目标
2. 增加群体间竞争
3. 进行同伴评估
4. 选择高动机且喜欢群体工作的成员
5. 基于每个成员的独特贡献设定群体奖励
6. 公开发布个人绩效评级

### 八、群体属性5：凝聚力 / Group Property 5: Cohesiveness

Cohesiveness is the degree to which group members are attracted to each other and are motivated to stay in the group.

凝聚力是群体成员彼此吸引并被激励留在群体中的程度。

**Relationship between Cohesiveness and Productivity**:

**凝聚力与生产力的关系**：

- **High Cohesiveness + High Performance Norms** → High productivity (最佳：高凝聚力+高绩效规范=高生产力)
- **High Cohesiveness + Low Performance Norms** → Low productivity (高凝聚力+低绩效规范=低生产力)
- **Low Cohesiveness + High Performance Norms** → Moderate productivity (低凝聚力+高绩效规范=中等生产力)
- **Low Cohesiveness + Low Performance Norms** → Low-to-moderate productivity (低凝聚力+低绩效规范=低到中等生产力)

**How to Encourage Cohesiveness**:
1. Make the group smaller
2. Encourage agreement with group goals
3. Increase time members spend together
4. Increase group status and membership difficulty
5. Stimulate competition with other groups
6. Give rewards to the group rather than individuals
7. Physically isolate the group

**如何促进凝聚力**：
1. 缩小群体规模
2. 鼓励认同群体目标
3. 增加成员共处时间
4. 提高群体地位和加入难度
5. 激发与其他群体的竞争
6. 奖励群体而非个人
7. 物理隔离群体

### 九、群体属性6：多元化 / Group Property 6: Diversity

**Surface-Level Diversity**: Observable characteristics such as national origin, race, and gender. May subconsciously cue team members to be more open-minded in their views.

**表层多元化**：可观察的特征，如国籍、种族和性别。可能在潜意识中提示团队成员在观点上更加开放。

**Deep-Level Diversity**: Underlying attitudes, values, and opinions. Effects are less understood but research shows putting people with different needs together can reduce unproductive competition.

**深层多元化**：潜在的态度、价值观和观点。影响尚不完全清楚，但研究表明将有不同需求的人放在一起可以减少无效竞争。

**Faultlines**: Perceived divisions that split groups into two or more subgroups based on individual differences such as sex, race, age, work experience, and education. Faultlines are generally detrimental — subgroups compete, learn more slowly, make riskier decisions, are less creative, and experience higher conflict. However, forced collaboration and common goals may transcend faultlines.

**断层线**：基于性别、种族、年龄、工作经验和教育等个体差异将群体分为两个或更多子群体的感知分裂。断层线通常是不利的——子群体竞争、学习更慢、做出更冒险的决策、创造力较低，并经历更高冲突。但是，强制合作和共同目标可能超越断层线。

### 十、群体决策 / Group Decision Making

**Strengths of Group Decision Making**:
- More complete information and knowledge
- Increased diversity of views
- More approaches and alternatives considered
- Increased acceptance of a solution

**群体决策的优势**：
- 更完整的信息和知识
- 增加观点的多样性
- 考虑更多方法和替代方案
- 增加对解决方案的接受度

**Weaknesses of Group Decision Making**:
- Time-consuming
- Conformity pressures
- Discussion can be dominated by one or a few members
- Ambiguous responsibility

**群体决策的劣势**：
- 耗时
- 从众压力
- 讨论可能被一个或少数成员主导
- 责任模糊

**Effectiveness and Efficiency**: Groups are generally more accurate than the average individual but less accurate than the most accurate person. Individuals are superior in speed. Groups are more effective for creativity and acceptance of solutions.

**效果和效率**：群体通常比平均个人更准确，但不如最准确的人。个人在速度上更优秀。群体在创造力和解决方案接受度方面更有效。

### 十一、群体思维 / Groupthink

Groupthink describes situations in which group pressures for conformity deter the group from critically appraising unusual, minority, or unpopular views. It occurs most often when there is a clear group identity, a positive image the group wants to protect, and a perceived collective threat.

群体思维描述了群体从众压力阻止群体批判性评估不寻常、少数或不受欢迎的观点的情况。它最常发生在有明确群体身份、群体想要保护的积极形象以及感知到的集体威胁时。

**Minimizing Groupthink**:
1. Monitor group size (less than 10 members)
2. Encourage leaders to be impartial
3. Appoint a devil's advocate
4. Use exercises that stimulate diverse alternatives
5. Have members discuss dangers/risks first before gains

**最小化群体思维**：
1. 监控群体规模（少于10人）
2. 鼓励领导者保持公正
3. 指定唱反调者
4. 使用激发多样替代方案的练习
5. 让成员先讨论危险/风险再讨论收益

### 十二、群体极化 / Groupshift or Group Polarization

Groupshift describes the way group members tend to exaggerate their initial positions when discussing alternatives. Conservatives become more cautious; aggressive types take on more risk. The group's decision reflects the dominant decision-making norm toward greater caution or more risk.

群体偏移描述了群体成员在讨论替代方案时倾向于夸大其初始立场的方式。保守派变得更加谨慎；激进派承担更多风险。群体的决策反映了主导的决策规范——更加谨慎或更多风险。

**Explanations for Polarization**:
1. Discussion makes members more comfortable expressing extreme views
2. The group diffuses responsibility — no single member is accountable
3. People want to demonstrate how different they are from the outgroup

**极化的解释**：
1. 讨论让成员更自在地表达极端观点
2. 群体分散了责任——没有单个成员负责
3. 人们想展示他们与外群体的差异

### 十三、群体决策技术 / Group Decision-Making Techniques

**Brainstorming**: Encourages any and all alternatives while withholding criticism. No criticism is allowed; all ideas are recorded. However, individuals working alone generate more ideas than a group in brainstorming due to "production blocking."

**头脑风暴**：鼓励任何和所有替代方案，同时保留批评。不允许批评；所有想法都被记录。然而，由于"生产阻碍"，单独工作的个人比头脑风暴中的群体产生更多想法。

**Nominal Group Technique**: Restricts discussion during the decision-making process. Steps:
1. Members independently write down ideas
2. Each member presents one idea (no discussion until all presented)
3. Group discusses ideas for clarity and evaluates
4. Members silently rank-order ideas; highest aggregate ranking determines final decision

**名义群体技术**：在决策过程中限制讨论。步骤：
1. 成员独立写下想法
2. 每个成员提出一个想法（在所有想法呈现前不讨论）
3. 群体讨论想法以澄清和评估
4. 成员沉默地对想法排序；最高总排序决定最终决策

Research generally shows nominal groups outperform brainstorming groups.

研究通常表明名义群体优于头脑风暴群体。

## 第三部分：考点总结 / Key Exam Points

### 🔴 高频考点（必考）

1. **间断-平衡模型 / Punctuated-Equilibrium Model**: Six stages, midpoint transition, periods of inertia. Applies to temporary groups with deadlines.
2. **角色概念 / Role Concepts**: Role perception, role expectations, psychological contract, role conflict, interrole conflict (work-family conflict).
3. **规范与从众 / Norms and Conformity**: Hawthorne Studies findings, Asch experiments (75% conformed at least once), reference groups.
4. **社会惰性与防止方法 / Social Loafing and Prevention**: Definition, Western bias vs. collectivistic societies, six prevention methods.
5. **凝聚力与生产力的关系 / Cohesiveness and Productivity Relationship**: Four combinations and their productivity outcomes.
6. **群体思维 / Groupthink**: Definition, conditions, five minimization strategies.
7. **群体偏移 / Groupshift**: Definition, explanation, shift toward extremes.

### 🟡 中频考点（常考）

1. **正式vs非正式群体 / Formal vs. Informal Groups**: Definitions and examples. 定义和示例。
2. **社会认同理论 / Social Identity Theory**: Ingroup favoritism, outgroup dynamics. 内群体偏袒，外群体动态。
3. **地位来源 / Status Sources**: Three determinants (power, contribution, personal characteristics). 三个决定因素（权力、贡献、个人特征）。
4. **断层线 / Faultlines**: Definition, negative effects, how to transcend them. 定义、负面效应、如何超越。
5. **群体决策优势vs劣势 / Strengths and Weaknesses of Group Decision Making**: Compare with individual decision making. 与个人决策比较。
6. **头脑风暴vs名义群体技术 / Brainstorming vs. Nominal Group Technique**: Process, advantages, limitations. 过程、优势、局限性。
7. **越轨工作行为四类 / Four Categories of Deviant Workplace Behavior**: Production, property, political, personal aggression. 生产、财产、政治、人身攻击。

### 🟢 低频考点（了解）

1. **地位与规范的关系 / Status and Norms**: High-status individuals resist conformity pressures. 高地位个体抵制从众压力。
2. **地位不平等的影响 / Effects of Status Inequity**: Resentment, poorer performance, higher turnover. 怨恨、绩效更差、离职率更高。
3. **表层vs深层多元化 / Surface-Level vs. Deep-Level Diversity**: Definitions and effects. 定义和影响。
4. **霍桑实验具体发现 / Specific Hawthorne Studies Findings**: Bank wiring observation room norms. 银行配线观察室规范。

#### 论述题 / Essay Question

**Q11:** Explain the relationship between group cohesiveness and performance norms. What strategies can managers use to encourage cohesiveness?

<details>
<summary>查看答案 / Answer Template</summary>

**Standard Answer Template / 标准答案模板:**

1. **Define Cohesiveness / 定义凝聚力**

   Cohesiveness is the degree to which group members are attracted to each other and motivated to stay in the group.

   凝聚力是群体成员彼此吸引并被激励留在群体中的程度。

2. **Four Combinations / 四种组合**

   High cohesiveness + high norms = high productivity. High cohesiveness + low norms = low productivity. Low cohesiveness + high norms = moderate productivity. Low cohesiveness + low norms = low-to-moderate productivity.

   高凝聚力+高规范=高生产力。高凝聚力+低规范=低生产力。低凝聚力+高规范=中等生产力。低凝聚力+低规范=低到中等生产力。

3. **Implications / 含义**

   The key insight is that cohesiveness alone does not guarantee high productivity. High performance norms are equally essential. A highly cohesive group with low norms will be unproductive because members strongly reinforce poor standards.

   关键见解是仅靠凝聚力不能保证高生产力。高绩效规范同样必不可少。具有高凝聚力但低规范的群体将是无生产力的，因为成员强烈强化低标准。

4. **Seven Strategies / 七种策略**

   (1) Make the group smaller. (2) Encourage agreement with group goals. (3) Increase time members spend together. (4) Increase group status and membership difficulty. (5) Stimulate competition with other groups. (6) Give rewards to the group rather than individuals. (7) Physically isolate the group.

   （1）缩小群体规模。（2）鼓励认同群体目标。（3）增加成员共处时间。（4）提高群体地位和加入难度。（5）激发与其他群体的竞争。（6）奖励群体而非个人。（7）物理隔离群体。

</details>

### 论述题答题模板 / Essay Answer Template

**Sample Question / 题目示例:** Discuss the strengths and weaknesses of group decision making. When are groups more effective than individuals?

**Standard Answer / 标准答案:**

1. **Strengths / 优势**

   Groups generate more complete information and knowledge. They offer increased diversity of views, opening the opportunity for more approaches and alternatives. Finally, groups lead to increased acceptance of a solution — members who participate are more likely to support and encourage others.

   群体产生更完整的信息和知识。它们提供更多样化的观点，为更多方法和替代方案创造机会。最后，群体导致更高的解决方案接受度——参与的成员更可能支持并鼓励他人。

2. **Weaknesses / 劣势**

   Group decisions are time-consuming. There are conformity pressures that squash overt disagreement. Discussion can be dominated by one or a few members. Finally, group decisions suffer from ambiguous responsibility — no single member is accountable.

   群体决策耗时。存在压制公开分歧的从众压力。讨论可能被一个或少数成员主导。最后，群体决策受困于责任模糊——没有单个成员负责。

3. **Effectiveness vs. Efficiency / 效果vs效率**

   Groups are generally more accurate than the average individual but less accurate than the most accurate person. Individuals are superior in speed. Groups are more effective for creativity and acceptance of solutions. However, groups consume more work hours than individuals.

   群体通常比平均个人更准确，但不如最准确的人。个人在速度上更优秀。群体在创造力和解决方案接受度方面更有效。但是，群体比个人消耗更多工作时间。

4. **When to Use Groups / 何时使用群体**

   Use groups when accuracy, creativity, and acceptance are more important than speed. Use individuals when speed and efficiency are priorities. Managers must assess whether increases in effectiveness offset reductions in efficiency.

   当准确性、创造力和接受度比速度更重要时使用群体。当速度和效率是优先事项时使用个人。管理者必须评估效果增加是否抵消效率降低。
