## 第一部分：理论基础 / Theory Foundations

### 一、组织文化定义与七特征 / Definition and Seven Characteristics

Organizational culture refers to a system of shared meaning held by members that distinguishes the organization from other organizations. It is a descriptive term (how employees perceive characteristics), not an evaluative one like job satisfaction (how employees feel).

组织文化指成员持有的共享意义体系，将组织与其他组织区分开来。它是描述性术语（员工如何感知特征），不是像工作满意度那样的评价性术语（员工感觉如何）。

**Seven Primary Characteristics (七个主要特征)**:

1. **Innovation and risk taking (创新和冒险)**: Degree employees are encouraged to be innovative and take risks.
   员工被鼓励创新和冒险的程度。

2. **Attention to detail (关注细节)**: Degree employees are expected to exhibit precision, analysis, and attention to detail.
   员工被期望展现精确、分析和关注细节的程度。

3. **Outcome orientation (结果导向)**: Degree management focuses on results rather than techniques and processes.
   管理层关注结果而非技术和过程的程度。

4. **People orientation (人员导向)**: Degree management decisions consider the effect on people within the organization.
   管理决策考虑对组织内人员影响的程度。

5. **Team orientation (团队导向)**: Degree work activities are organized around teams rather than individuals.
   工作活动围绕团队而非个人组织的程度。

6. **Aggressiveness (进取性)**: Degree people are aggressive and competitive rather than easygoing.
   人们积极进取和好竞争而非随和的程度。

7. **Stability (稳定性)**: Degree organizational activities emphasize maintaining status quo versus growth.
   组织活动强调维持现状而非增长的程度。

### 二、主导文化与亚文化 / Dominant Culture and Subcultures

**Dominant Culture (主导文化)**: Expresses the core values shared by a majority of members. Gives the organization its distinct personality. Example: Zappos values customer care and dedication over speed and efficiency.
表达多数成员共享的核心价值观。赋予组织独特个性。

**Core Values (核心价值观)**: The primary or dominant values accepted throughout the organization.
整个组织接受的主要或主导价值观。

**Subcultures (亚文化)**: Develop in large organizations in response to common problems or experiences a group faces. Defined by department designations and geographical separation.
在大型组织中因群体面临的共同问题或经历而发展。由部门指定和地理分离定义。

### 三、文化的创建与维持 / Creating and Sustaining Culture

**How Culture Begins (文化如何开始)**:

Founders hire and keep employees who think and feel the way they do. Founders socialize employees to their way of thinking and feeling. Founders' own behavior acts as a role model. Three ways founders imprint culture:
1. Founders hire employees who think and feel as they do.
2. Founders indoctrinate and socialize employees to their way of thinking and feeling.
3. Founders' behavior acts as a role model that encourages employees to identify with and internalize beliefs.

创始人雇用和保留与他们想法和感受相同的员工。创始人向员工灌输他们的思维和感受方式。创始人自己的行为充当角色模型。

**How Culture is Sustained (文化如何维持)**:

The selection process, performance evaluation criteria, training and development activities, and promotion procedures ensure employees fit the culture. Top management establishes behavioral norms.
选拔过程、绩效评估标准、培训发展活动和晋升程序确保员工适合文化。高层管理者建立行为规范。

**Three Socialization Stages (三个社会化阶段)**:

1. **Prearrival Stage (到达前阶段)**: Everything learned before joining the organization. The selection process is used to inform prospective employees about the organization as a whole.
   加入组织前学到的一切。选拔过程用于向潜在员工告知整个组织的信息。

2. **Encounter Stage (相遇阶段)**: New employee sees what the organization is really like and confronts possibility that expectations and reality may diverge. If expectations don't match reality, the newcomer must undergo socialization.
   新员工看到组织的真实面貌，并面对期望与现实可能不同的可能性。如果期望与现实不匹配，新来者必须经历社会化。

3. **Metamorphosis Stage (蜕变阶段)**: Newcomer has worked out problems and undergone change. Better the newcomer's adaptation, the higher productivity and commitment and lower intention to turnover.
   新来者已解决问题并经历变化。新来者适应越好，生产力和承诺越高，离职意向越低。

### 四、文化如何传递给员工 / How Culture is Transmitted

Culture is transmitted to employees in several forms:

**Stories (故事)**: Narratives based on events that frequently include key people, challenges, and outcomes. Example: Nordstrom's customer service stories.
基于事件的叙述，经常包括关键人物、挑战和结果。

**Rituals (仪式)**: Repetitive sequences of activities that express and reinforce the key values of the organization. Example: Walmart's cheer.
表达和强化组织关键价值观的重复活动序列。

**Material Symbols (物质符号)**: Office layout, top management behavior, dress code, perks, furnishings. Open offices, casual dress, and flextime communicate informality.
办公室布局、高管行为、着装规范、福利、家具。

**Language (语言)**: Specialized terms, acronyms, and jargon that identify culture members. Once learned, language acts as a common denominator that unites members.
专用术语、缩略语和行话识别文化成员。

### 五、创建道德文化、积极文化和精神文化 / Creating Ethical, Positive, and Spiritual Cultures

**Ethical Culture (道德文化)**:
- High risk tolerance, control, and conflict tolerance
- Realistic outcomes from performance appraisals
- Reward systems that recognize both means and ends
- Managers serve as visible role models
- Organizations with ethics are more likely to have a code of ethics, ethics training, public reporting mechanisms

高冒险容忍度、控制、冲突容忍度
绩效评估的现实结果
认可手段和结果的奖励系统
管理者作为可见的角色模型

**Positive Organizational Culture (积极组织文化)**:
- Leaders emphasize building on employee strengths rather than dwelling on mistakes
- Reward more than punish — recognize positive achievements
- Emphasize vitality and growth — help employees fulfill natural potential for growth, development, and accomplishment
- Research shows positive cultures associated with more positive emotions, less stress, better performance

领导者强调建立在员工优势上而非纠结于错误
奖励多于惩罚——认可积极成就
强调活力和成长——帮助员工实现成长、发展和成就的自然潜力

**Spirituality and Organizational Culture (精神性与组织文化)**:
- Organizational spirituality is a culture where people have an inner life that nourishes and is nourished by meaningful work in the context of community.
- Not about organized religious practices. It recognizes that people have an inner life that nourishes and is nourished by meaningful work.
- Characteristics: strong sense of purpose, focus on trust and respect, open-mindedness, recognition that work serves a higher purpose

组织精神性是一种文化，人们有内在生活，在有意义的社区工作背景下得到滋养和滋养。
不是关于有组织的宗教实践。它承认人们有内在生活，被有意义的工作滋养。

### 六、民族文化对组织文化的影响 / National Culture's Effect on Organizational Culture

National culture affects the way organizational culture is interpreted. Even if an organization tries to establish the same organizational culture in another country, the national culture of that country will influence how the organizational culture is interpreted.
民族文化影响组织文化的解释方式。即使组织试图在另一个国家建立相同的组织文化，该国的民族文化也会影响组织文化的解释。

- Organizational cultures can be stronger than national cultures (e.g., Japanese companies in the United States).
- However, when organizations operate with people from different national cultures, employees may perceive and interpret the organizational culture differently.
- Global organizations must consider both national and organizational cultures when making decisions.

组织文化可能比民族文化更强大（例如，在美国的日本公司）。
然而，当组织与来自不同民族文化的人合作时，员工可能以不同方式感知和解释组织文化。
全球组织在做决策时必须同时考虑民族文化和组织文化。

4. **民族文化对组织文化的影响 / National Culture's Effect**: Interpretation differences
