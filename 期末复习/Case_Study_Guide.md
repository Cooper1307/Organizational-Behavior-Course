## 第一部分：案例分析通用方法 / Universal Case Analysis Method

### 四步分析法 / Four-Step Analysis Framework

**Step 1: 识别问题 / Identify the Problem**
- What is the core OB issue? (核心OB问题是什么？)
- Look for keywords: motivation, conflict, leadership, communication
- 寻找关键词：动机、冲突、领导力、沟通

**Step 2: 选择理论 / Select Relevant Theory**
- Match case details to OB theories
- 将案例细节与OB理论匹配
- Common theories: Expectancy, Equity, Reinforcement, Goal-Setting, Big Five, Transformational Leadership

**Step 3: 应用理论 / Apply Theory**
- Define theory with terminology
- 用专业术语定义理论
- Connect theory to case details
- 将理论与案例细节联系
- Use specific examples from case
- 使用案例中的具体例子

**Step 4: 提出建议 / Recommend Solutions**
- Based on theory, what should manager do?
- 基于理论，管理者应该做什么？
- Provide actionable recommendations
- 提供可操作的建议

## 第二部分：案例一 — Digital Systems / Case 1: Digital Systems

### 案例背景 / Case Background

**公司 / Company**: Digital Systems (零售企业)

**CEO**: John Anderson

**核心事件 / Key Events**:
1. 2024年公司业绩不佳 → 2025年1月推出激励性薪酬计划
2. Q1 (1-3月) 超过75%门店经理获得奖金资格
3. CEO认为目标过低 → 提高Q2要求 + 取消Q1已获资格奖金

**核心问题 / Core Issues**:
- 激励计划是否有效？
- CEO取消已承诺奖金的后果是什么？

---

### 问题1解析 / Question 1 Analysis

**题目 / Question**: Do you think the store managers were motivated by the incentive plan? Explain using expectancy, equity, or reinforcement theory.

#### 答案A：期望理论 / Answer A: Expectancy Theory

**Theory Applied**: Expectancy Theory — Victor Vroom (1964)

**标准答案 / Standard Answer**:

Based on **Expectancy Theory** (期望理论), I believe the store managers were indeed motivated by the incentive plan. Expectancy Theory proposes that motivation is determined by three relationships: (1) **Effort-Performance Expectancy** (努力-绩效期望) — the belief that effort will lead to performance; (2) **Performance-Reward Instrumentality** (绩效-奖励工具性) — the belief that performance will lead to rewards; and (3) **Reward-Personal Valence** (奖励-个人效价) — the value the individual places on the reward.

In this case, the fact that **over 75 percent of store managers qualified for the bonus** demonstrates strong motivation. The **Effort-Performance** link was high because the performance goals appeared achievable — managers believed their effort could lead to goal attainment. The **Performance-Reward** link was clear because the incentive plan explicitly stated that achieving performance goals would result in quarterly bonuses. The **Valence** was also high because monetary bonuses are typically valued by employees.

However, the extremely high success rate (75%) also suggests that the performance goals were set **too easy**, which relates to **Goal-Setting Theory** — goals that are too easy may generate compliance but not optimal performance improvement.

**中文翻译**:

基于期望理论，我认为门店经理确实受到了激励性计划的激励。期望理论提出，动机由三种关系决定：（1）努力-绩效期望——相信努力能带来绩效；（2）绩效-奖励工具性——相信绩效能带来奖励；（3）奖励-个人效价——个人对奖励价值的重视程度。

在本案例中，超过75%的门店经理获得奖金资格的事实证明了强烈的动机。努力-绩效联系很高，因为绩效目标看起来是可以实现的。绩效-奖励联系很明确，因为激励计划明确说明达成业绩目标将获得季度奖金。效价也很高，因为金钱奖励通常受到员工重视。

然而，极高的成功率（75%）也表明绩效目标设定得太容易，这涉及到目标设置理论——过于容易的目标可能只会产生顺从而非最佳的绩效提升。

---

#### 答案B：公平理论 / Answer B: Equity Theory

**Theory Applied**: Equity Theory — J. Stacy Adams (1963)

**标准答案 / Standard Answer**:

From an **Equity Theory** (公平理论) perspective, the store managers were motivated because they perceived **equity** (公平) in the reward system. Equity Theory states that employees compare their **Input-Output Ratio** (投入产出比) to that of referent others. When the ratio is perceived as fair, employees are motivated to maintain their effort level.

In this case, the incentive plan created a clear and transparent link between inputs (effort, performance) and outputs (quarterly bonuses). The fact that 75% of managers qualified suggests that the system was perceived as **achievable and fair**, which motivated managers to put in the necessary effort.

---

### 问题2解析 / Question 2 Analysis

**题目 / Question**: What will happen to motivation in Quarter 2? Why?

#### 答案A：期望理论 / Answer A: Expectancy Theory

**标准答案 / Standard Answer**:

I believe the store managers' motivation in Quarter 2 will **significantly decrease** (显著下降). Using **Expectancy Theory**, we can analyze why:

First, the **Performance-Reward Instrumentality** (绩效-奖励工具性) has been **destroyed**. When the CEO retroactively cancelled the bonuses that managers had already earned, he broke the trust that performance leads to rewards. Managers will no longer believe that achieving goals will actually result in receiving bonuses — **Instrumentality has dropped to near zero**.

Second, the **Effort-Performance Expectancy** (努力-绩效期望) may also decrease because the CEO raised the performance requirements, making goals appear more difficult or even unattainable.

Third, the **Valence** (效价) of the bonus may also decrease because managers now view the reward system as **unreliable and unfair**.

According to Vroom's formula **Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence**, when any of these three factors approaches zero, overall motivation approaches zero.

**中文翻译**:

我认为门店经理在第二季度的动机将显著下降。使用期望理论，我们可以分析原因：

首先，绩效-奖励工具性已被摧毁。当CEO追溯性地取消了经理们已赚得的奖金时，他破坏了"绩效会带来奖励"的信任。经理们将不再相信达成目标会真正获得奖金——工具性已降至接近零。

其次，努力-绩效期望也可能下降，因为CEO提高了绩效要求，使目标看起来更加困难甚至无法达成。

第三，奖金的效价也可能下降，因为经理们现在将奖励体系视为"不可靠且不公平"。

根据Vroom的公式"动机=期望×工具性×效价"，当这三个因素中的任何一个接近零时，整体动机就接近零。

---

#### 答案B：强化理论 / Answer B: Reinforcement Theory

**Theory Applied**: Reinforcement Theory — B.F. Skinner

**标准答案 / Standard Answer**:

According to **Reinforcement Theory** (强化理论), the store managers' motivation in Quarter 2 will **collapse**. Reinforcement Theory explains behavior through **consequences** — behaviors followed by positive consequences are more likely to be repeated, while behaviors followed by negative consequences are less likely to be repeated.

In Quarter 1, the managers' high performance was followed by the **promise** of bonuses, which should have served as **positive reinforcement** (正强化). However, the CEO's decision to **retroactively cancel** the bonuses constitutes **punishment** (惩罚) — a negative consequence applied despite meeting the stated requirements.

Furthermore, this creates a situation of **Extinction** (消退) — if managers put in high effort in Quarter 2 but no longer expect rewards, the previously reinforced behavior (high performance) will gradually disappear.

## 第三部分：案例二 — Gulf Shore Software / Case 2: Gulf Shore Software

### 案例背景 / Case Background

**公司 / Company**: Gulf Shore Software Inc. (小型软件公司，酒店餐饮行业软件)

**CEO**: Elizabeth Green (创始人，3年历史，刚开始盈利)

**核心问题 / Core Problem**: 4名员工不满，2人考虑离职，需要激励方案

**激励计划 / Incentive Plan**: $1000现金奖金，按时交付定制软件

---

### 员工分析 / Employee Analysis

#### 员工1：Bill Jones (49岁，退休海军军官)

**个人特征 / Personal Characteristics**:
- 追求认可和成就（海军经历）
- 完美主义者，技术精湛
- 经济独立（退休金够用），钱主要用于个人爱好
- 感到无挑战、不被欣赏
- 传言可能离职去钓鱼旅行

**最佳解释理论 / Best Theory**: **McClelland's Need Theory (麦克利兰需求理论)**

**理论应用 / Theory Application**:

Bill has a high **need for achievement (nAch)** and **need for affiliation (nAff)**. McClelland's theory states that individuals have three primary needs: need for achievement (desire to excel), need for affiliation (desire for friendly relationships), and need for power (desire to control others).

Bill's military background satisfied his nAch through recognition opportunities. Now at Gulf Shore, he feels **unchallenged and unappreciated**, indicating his nAch is not being met. The $1000 bonus has **low valence** (效价低) for him because he doesn't need the money.

**Recommendation / 建议**:
1. Provide challenging projects that allow him to demonstrate expertise
2. Offer public recognition and appreciation for his perfectionism
3. Create mentorship role to utilize his leadership experience

---

#### 员工2：Sara Brown (17岁，高中辍学)

**个人特征 / Personal Characteristics**:
- 天赋程序员，自学成才
- 负责公司所有设备维护（唯一能做的人）
- 加班最多，工资最低（因为没有学历）
- 感觉被利用
- **热爱工作，不考虑离职**

**最佳解释理论 / Best Theory**: **Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory (赫茨伯格双因素理论) + Equity Theory**

**理论应用 / Theory Application**:

Sara's situation can be explained by **Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory**. The **motivators** (激励因素) — the work itself, recognition, responsibility — are present, which explains why she loves her work. However, the **hygiene factors** (保健因素) — pay equity, recognition for her unique contributions — are lacking.

From **Equity Theory** perspective, Sara perceives **under-reward inequity** (报酬不足不公平). Her inputs (skills, overtime, unique expertise) far exceed her outputs (lowest pay due to no degree). Despite this, her high job satisfaction from the work itself prevents her from leaving.

**Recommendation / 建议**:
1. Increase pay to reflect her actual contributions and unique expertise
2. Provide formal training/certification opportunities
3. Recognize her critical role in company operations
4. Reduce overtime burden by hiring IT support staff

---

#### 员工3：Katherine Wright (37岁，单亲母亲，3个孩子)

**个人特征 / Personal Characteristics**:
- 急需钱（养家）
- 编程速度慢，但客户关系好
- 办公室"胶水"（维护运转、保密）
- CEO的倾诉对象（外向的CEO需要"大声思考"）
- 认为目标几乎无法实现（时间被非编程工作占用）

**最佳解释理论 / Best Theory**: **Expectancy Theory (期望理论)**

**理论应用 / Theory Application**:

Katherine's motivation is **low** according to Expectancy Theory. Her **Effort-Performance Expectancy** (努力-绩效期望) is **very low** because she realistically assesses that she cannot achieve the programming goal given all the non-programming demands on her time (customer service, office management, emotional support for CEO).

Her **Valence** (效价) for the bonus is **very high** because she desperately needs money, but the **Expectancy** is so low that overall motivation remains low. According to Vroom's formula: **Low Expectancy × High Valence = Low Motivation**.

**Recommendation / 建议**:
1. Separate her role into customer service/admin vs. programming
2. Create different performance metrics for her actual contributions
3. Provide support for her non-programming responsibilities
4. Consider part-time customer service role with different bonus structure

---

#### 员工4：Ron Hall (25岁，双学位，单身)

**个人特征 / Personal Characteristics**:
- 双学位（营销+MIS），来自大型电脑公司
- 喜欢挑战和客户社交
- 销售业绩因他而增长
- 认为自己应该最高薪（双学位、销售贡献）
- 发现Bill（哲学学位）比他赚更多 → 不满

**最佳解释理论 / Best Theory**: **Equity Theory (公平理论) + McClelland's Need Theory**

**理论应用 / Theory Application**:

Ron's situation is a classic example of **Equity Theory** — **under-reward inequity** (报酬不足不公平). He compares his **input-output ratio** to Bill Jones':

- **Ron's inputs**: 2 degrees, sales increase, customer networking
- **Ron's outputs**: Lower pay than Bill
- **Bill's inputs**: 20-year-old philosophy degree, no sales contribution
- **Bill's outputs**: Higher pay

Ron perceives this as **unfair** and is considering leaving. Additionally, McClelland's theory shows Ron has high **need for achievement** and **need for recognition**, both of which are currently unmet despite his sales success.

**Recommendation / 建议**:
1. Restructure pay to reflect actual contributions (sales-based bonuses)
2. Create separate career track for sales vs. programming roles
3. Provide public recognition for sales achievements
4. Offer equity/profit-sharing to increase ownership feeling

## 第五部分：跨案例分析框架 / Cross-Case Analysis Framework

### Digital Systems vs. Gulf Shore Software 对比

| 维度 / Dimension | Digital Systems | Gulf Shore Software |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **公司规模** | 大型零售企业 | 小型软件公司（4名员工） |
| **核心问题** | 激励计划设计不当 + 信任破坏 | 个性化激励不足 |
| **理论应用** | Expectancy, Reinforcement, Goal-Setting | Expectancy, Equity, McClelland, Herzberg |
| **管理错误** | 追溯取消奖金、提高目标不沟通 | 一刀切奖金计划、未考虑个体差异 |
| **关键教训** | 信任是激励的基础 | 激励必须个性化 |

### 通用OB原则 / Universal OB Principles

1. **激励是个性化的** / Motivation is Individualized
   - 不同员工有不同需求（Bill vs. Katherine vs. Sara vs. Ron）
   - 一刀切的激励计划注定失败

2. **信任是激励的基础** / Trust is the Foundation of Motivation
   - Digital Systems CEO破坏信任 → 动机崩溃
   - Gulf Shore Elizabeth需要建立透明公平的体系

3. **理论必须结合实践** / Theory Must Connect to Practice
   - 期望理论三要素必须同时满足
   - 公平理论要求投入产出比合理
   - 强化理论要求及时、一致的后果

4. **沟通是关键** / Communication is Key
   - 目标变更必须沟通（Digital Systems）
   - 角色期望必须明确（Gulf Shore）
