### 题目1：人格与工作绩效 / Question 1: Personality and Job Performance (35分)

**题目 / Question**:

Discuss how personality affects workplace behavior. Use the Big Five model to explain which traits are most predictive of job performance, and discuss how organizations can use personality assessments in hiring and team building.

讨论人格如何影响工作场所行为。使用大五模型解释哪些特质最能预测工作绩效，并讨论组织如何在招聘和团队建设中利用人格评估。

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Personality and Big Five (定义人格和大五)**

Personality is the sum total of ways an individual reacts and interacts with others, determined by heredity, environment, and situation. The **Big Five model** (大五模型) identifies five key dimensions: **Openness** (开放性), **Conscientiousness** (尽责性), **Extraversion** (外向性), **Agreeableness** (宜人性), and **Emotional Stability** (情绪稳定性).

**Paragraph 2: Conscientiousness as Strongest Predictor (尽责性作为最强预测因子)**

Research consistently shows that **Conscientiousness** is the strongest predictor of job performance across all jobs. Conscientious individuals are responsible, dependable, persistent, and organized. They set high standards for themselves and work diligently to achieve them. This trait predicts performance because conscientious employees are more likely to complete tasks on time, maintain quality standards, and persist through challenges.

**Paragraph 3: Other Traits and Their Predictions (其他特质及其预测)**

**Extraversion** is most predictive of leadership emergence and performance in social jobs (sales, management). **Openness** predicts training proficiency and adaptability to change. **Emotional Stability** predicts stress tolerance and overall job satisfaction. **Agreeableness** predicts teamwork effectiveness but may reduce performance in competitive situations.

**Paragraph 4: Personality in Hiring (招聘中的人格应用)**

Organizations can use personality assessments in hiring through: (a) Selecting candidates whose personality matches job requirements (person-job fit). (b) Using validated instruments like NEO-PI-R for Big Five measurement. (c) Creating personality profiles for high performers and using them as benchmarks. However, organizations must avoid discriminating based on personality and ensure assessments are job-related.

**Paragraph 5: Personality in Team Building (团队建设中的人格应用)**

In team building, organizations should: (a) Avoid mixing personality levels within teams (research shows low-conscientiousness members drag down high-conscientiousness members). (b) Match personality compositions to task types (creative tasks benefit from high openness; routine tasks benefit from high conscientiousness). (c) Use personality awareness to improve communication and reduce conflict. (d) Remember that personality is not destiny — training and development can help individuals adapt.

**Conclusion**: Personality significantly affects workplace behavior, with conscientiousness being the strongest performance predictor. Organizations should use personality assessments thoughtfully in hiring and team building while respecting individual differences.

</details>

### 题目2：知觉偏差与决策 / Question 2: Perceptual Biases and Decision Making (35分)

**题目 / Question**:

Explain the common perceptual biases and decision-making errors that affect managers. How can organizations help managers make better decisions despite these biases?

解释影响管理者的常见知觉偏差和决策错误。组织如何帮助管理者在存在这些偏差的情况下做出更好的决策？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Perception and Its Importance (定义知觉及其重要性)**

**Perception** (知觉) is the process by which individuals organize and interpret sensory impressions to give meaning to their environment. Because people behave based on their perception of reality rather than reality itself, understanding perceptual biases is crucial for managers.

**Paragraph 2: Attribution Errors (归因错误)**

The **fundamental attribution error** (基本归因错误) is the tendency to underestimate external factors and overestimate internal factors when judging others. The **self-serving bias** (自利偏差) is the tendency to attribute one's own successes to internal factors and failures to external factors. These biases lead managers to blame employees for problems while taking credit for successes.

**Paragraph 3: Judgment Shortcuts (判断捷径)**

Common shortcuts include: **Selective perception** (选择性知觉) — selectively interpreting based on one's interests; **Halo effect** (晕轮效应) — drawing general impressions from a single characteristic; **Contrast effect** (对比效应) — evaluating a person by comparing them to others recently encountered; **Stereotyping** (刻板印象) — judging someone based on one's perception of the group they belong to.

**Paragraph 4: Decision Biases (决策偏差)**

Key biases include: **Overconfidence bias** (过度自信偏差) — overestimating accuracy of own judgments; **Anchoring bias** (锚定偏差) — fixating on initial information; **Confirmation bias** (确认偏差) — selectively gathering confirming information; **Escalation of commitment** (承诺升级) — increasing commitment despite negative evidence; **Hindsight bias** (后见之明偏差) — believing after the fact that one predicted the outcome.

**Paragraph 5: Improving Decision Quality (提高决策质量)**

Organizations can help managers by: (a) Training managers to recognize their own biases. (b) Using structured decision-making processes that require considering alternatives. (c) Encouraging devil's advocate perspectives to challenge assumptions. (d) Seeking disconfirming evidence. (e) Using data and analytics to supplement intuition. (f) Creating a culture that acknowledges mistakes and learns from them.

**Conclusion**: Perceptual biases and decision errors are inevitable but manageable. Organizations that invest in bias awareness and structured decision processes will make better decisions.

</details>

### 题目3：情绪管理与工作表现 / Question 3: Emotion Management and Job Performance (30分)

**题目 / Question**:

Discuss the concept of emotional labor and explain the difference between surface acting and deep acting. How can organizations help employees manage emotional labor effectively?

讨论情绪劳动的概念，解释表层扮演和深层扮演的区别。组织如何帮助员工有效管理情绪劳动？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Emotional Labor (定义情绪劳动)**

**Emotional labor** (情绪劳动) is an employee's expression of organizationally desired emotions during interpersonal transactions at work. It is particularly relevant in service jobs where employees must display specific emotions to customers (friendliness, enthusiasm, empathy).

**Paragraph 2: Surface Acting vs. Deep Acting (表层扮演vs深层扮演)**

**Surface acting** (表层扮演) is hiding one's inner feelings and forgoing emotional expressions in response to display rules — the employee puts on a false face. **Deep acting** (深层扮演) is trying to modify one's true inner feelings based on display rules — the employee actually tries to experience the emotion required. The key difference is that surface acting deals with displayed emotions while deep acting deals with felt emotions.

**Paragraph 3: Effectiveness Comparison (有效性比较)**

Deep acting is more effective and less psychologically costly than surface acting. Surface acting creates **emotional dissonance** (情绪失调) — inconsistency between felt and displayed emotions — which leads to emotional exhaustion, burnout, and reduced job satisfaction. Deep acting reduces this psychological burden because employees are not suppressing genuine emotions, creating more authentic emotional displays.

**Paragraph 4: Organizational Interventions (组织干预措施)**

Organizations can help employees by: (a) Selecting employees with high **emotional intelligence** (情绪智力) for jobs requiring high emotional labor. (b) Providing training in deep acting techniques and emotion regulation. (c) Creating a supportive work environment that allows employees to express genuine emotions during breaks. (d) Using mindfulness programs to help employees evaluate their emotional situations objectively. (e) Designing jobs that minimize the gap between felt and displayed emotions. (f) Monitoring for signs of emotional exhaustion and burnout.

**Conclusion**: Emotional labor is a real and significant workplace demand. Organizations that invest in employee emotional competence and provide supportive environments will see better customer service and lower turnover.

</details>

### 题目4：群体决策vs个人决策 / Question 4: Group Decision Making vs. Individual Decision Making (35分)

**题目 / Question**:

Compare and contrast group decision making with individual decision making. What are the strengths and weaknesses of groups? When are groups more effective than individuals?

比较群体决策与个人决策。群体的优势和劣势是什么？群体在什么时候比个人更有效？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Group Decision Making (定义群体决策)**

**Group decision making** (群体决策) occurs when two or more individuals interact to identify problems, generate alternatives, and select solutions. It differs from individual decision making in terms of information processing, creativity, speed, and acceptance.

**Paragraph 2: Strengths of Groups (群体优势)**

Groups have several strengths: (a) **More complete information** — groups bring together diverse knowledge and perspectives. (b) **Increased diversity of views** — multiple approaches and alternatives are generated. (c) **Higher acceptance of solutions** — members who participate in decision making are more likely to support and encourage others to accept the solution. (d) **Greater legitimacy** — group decisions are perceived as more democratic and fair.

**Paragraph 3: Weaknesses of Groups (群体劣势)**

Groups also have weaknesses: (a) **Time-consuming** — groups take longer to reach decisions. (b) **Conformity pressures** — the desire to be accepted can squash overt disagreement. (c) **Domination by one or few members** — discussion can be dominated by vocal members. (d) **Ambiguous responsibility** — no single member is accountable for the outcome. (e) **Groupthink** (群体思维) — conformity pressures deter critical appraisal of minority views. (f) **Groupshift** (群体偏移) — group decisions exaggerate initial positions toward extremes.

**Paragraph 4: When Groups Are More Effective (群体更有效的情况)**

Groups are more effective than individuals when: (a) The task is complex and requires diverse expertise. (b) Acceptance of the decision is critical for implementation. (c) Creativity and innovation are valued. (d) There is sufficient time for deliberation. (e) The group size is manageable (5-9 members). (f) The group has clear goals and norms.

**Paragraph 5: Improving Group Decision Quality (提高群体决策质量)**

To improve group decision quality: (a) Use **nominal group technique** (名义群体技术) — independent writing and ranking of ideas. (b) Encourage devil's advocate perspectives. (c) Bring in outside experts. (d) Use structured brainstorming with no criticism. (e) Break large groups into subgroups. (f) Establish norms that value dissenting opinions.

**Conclusion**: Groups and individuals each have advantages. Organizations should match the decision method to the task requirements, time constraints, and need for acceptance.

</details>

### 题目5：有效团队的特征 / Question 5: Characteristics of Effective Teams (35分)

**题目 / Question**:

Discuss the characteristics of effective teams. What contextual, compositional, and process factors contribute to team effectiveness? How can organizations turn individuals into team players?

讨论有效团队的特征。哪些背景、组成和过程因素促进团队有效性？组织如何将个人变成团队成员？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Effective Teams (定义有效团队)**

An **effective team** (有效团队) is one that achieves its performance goals, satisfies member needs, and maintains its viability over time. Effective teams share common characteristics organized into three categories: **contextual factors** (背景因素), **compositional factors** (组成因素), and **process variables** (过程变量).

**Paragraph 2: Contextual Factors (背景因素)**

Four contextual factors: (a) **Adequate resources** — timely information, proper equipment, adequate staffing, and organizational support. (b) **Leadership and structure** — agreement on who does what, shared workload, and clear direction. (c) **Climate of trust** — foundation of effective leadership; facilitates cooperation and reduces need for monitoring. (d) **Performance evaluation and reward system** — hybrid systems that balance individual and group contributions prevent social loafing while promoting teamwork.

**Paragraph 3: Composition Factors (组成因素)**

Key composition factors: (a) **Abilities** — high-ability teams perform better on complex problems requiring judgment. (b) **Personality** — conscientiousness is most important; teams should not mix high and low levels. (c) **Roles** — nine potential roles; put best workers in central roles. (d) **Diversity** — functional and educational diversity has positive effects. (e) **Size** — 5-9 members optimal ("two-pizza rule"). (f) **Member preferences** — select members who prefer teamwork.

**Paragraph 4: Process Factors (过程因素)**

Key process factors: (a) **Common plan and purpose** — with reflexivity (reflecting on and adjusting purpose). (b) **Specific, measurable, challenging goals** — facilitate clear direction. (c) **Team efficacy** — collective belief that team can succeed; built through small successes. (d) **Team identity and cohesion** — emotional connection and attachment. (e) **Shared mental models** — accurate representations of knowledge. (f) **Moderate task conflict, low relationship conflict** — task conflict stimulates creativity; relationship conflict is dysfunctional. (g) **Individual and joint accountability** — prevents social loafing.

**Paragraph 5: Turning Individuals into Team Players (将个人变成团队成员)**

Three approaches: (a) **Selecting** — hire candidates who have interpersonal skills and prefer teamwork. (b) **Training** — conduct workshops for problem-solving, communication, negotiation, and team dynamics. (c) **Rewarding** — create hybrid reward systems that balance individual and team contributions; use team-based bonuses, profit-sharing, and peer evaluations.

**Conclusion**: Effective teams require attention to context, composition, and process. Organizations must select, train, and reward team players systematically.

</details>

### 题目6：团队冲突管理 / Question 6: Team Conflict Management (30分)

**题目 / Question**:

Discuss the three types of conflict in teams and explain how managers can manage conflict effectively. When is conflict functional versus dysfunctional?

讨论团队中的三种冲突类型，解释管理者如何有效管理冲突。冲突在什么时候是功能性的，什么时候是功能失调的？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Conflict (定义冲突)**

**Conflict** (冲突) is a process that begins when one party perceives that another party has negatively affected, or is about to negatively affect, something the first party cares about. In teams, conflict is not always bad — moderate levels can be beneficial.

**Paragraph 2: Three Types of Conflict (三种冲突类型)**

**Relationship conflict** (关系冲突) focuses on interpersonal relationships, personality clashes, and emotional disagreements. It is almost always dysfunctional because it increases hostility and reduces trust. **Task conflict** (任务冲突) focuses on disagreements about work content and goals. Moderate levels are beneficial because they stimulate discussion, creativity, and critical evaluation. **Process conflict** (过程冲突) focuses on how work gets done — delegation and roles. It is usually dysfunctional, but low levels early in a project can clarify responsibilities.

**Paragraph 3: Five Conflict-Handling Intentions (五种冲突处理意图)**

Based on assertiveness and cooperativeness: (a) **Competing** (竞争) — assertive, uncooperative; pursuing own goals at others' expense. (b) **Collaborating** (合作) — assertive, cooperative; seeking win-win solutions. (c) **Avoiding** (回避) — unassertive, uncooperative; withdrawing from conflict. (d) **Accommodating** (迁就) — unassertive, cooperative; placing others' interests above own. (e) **Compromising** (妥协) — midrange on both dimensions; each party gives up something. Collaborating is generally the best approach; competing and avoiding are the worst.

**Paragraph 4: When Conflict is Functional vs. Dysfunctional (功能性vs功能失调)**

Conflict is **functional** (功能性) when: (a) It is task-related rather than personal. (b) It is at moderate levels. (c) It stimulates creativity and innovation. (d) It releases tensions and emotions. (e) It fosters an environment of self-evaluation and change. Conflict is **dysfunctional** (功能失调) when: (a) It is relationship-based. (b) It creates hostility and reduces trust. (c) It reduces group cohesiveness. (d) It subordinates group goals to infighting. (e) It stops communication and coordination.

**Paragraph 5: Managerial Strategies (管理策略)**

Managers can manage conflict effectively by: (a) Recognizing real disagreements (different verbiage for same issue). (b) Encouraging open, frank discussions focused on interests rather than positions. (c) Emphasizing shared interests and superordinate goals. (d) Using collaborative or compromising approaches. (e) Matching conflict-handling style to situation. (f) For too little conflict: bring in outsiders, appoint devil's advocate, restructure teams. (g) For too much conflict: use avoiding or accommodating temporarily, emphasize shared goals.

**Conclusion**: Conflict is inevitable and not inherently bad. Managers should stimulate task conflict at moderate levels while minimizing relationship conflict.

</details>

### 题目7：变革型领导vs交易型领导 / Question 7: Transformational vs. Transactional Leadership (35分)

**题目 / Question**:

Compare and contrast transformational leadership with transactional leadership. How do they complement each other? What evidence supports the effectiveness of transformational leadership?

比较变革型领导与交易型领导。它们如何互补？什么证据支持变革型领导的有效性？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Transactional Leadership (定义交易型领导)**

**Transactional leaders** (交易型领导者) guide followers toward established goals by clarifying role and task requirements. Their behaviors include: **contingent reward** (权变奖励) — exchanging rewards for effort; **management by exception active** (积极例外管理) — monitoring for deviations; **management by exception passive** (消极例外管理) — intervening only after standards not met; and **laissez-faire** (放任型) — avoiding responsibility and decisions.

**Paragraph 2: Define Transformational Leadership (定义变革型领导)**

**Transformational leaders** (变革型领导者) inspire followers to transcend their self-interests for the good of the organization. The **four I's** (四个I): **Idealized influence** (理想化影响) — providing vision and mission, instilling pride; **Inspirational motivation** (鼓舞性激励) — communicating high expectations, using symbols to focus efforts; **Intellectual stimulation** (智力刺激) — promoting rationality and problem solving; **Individualized consideration** (个性化关怀) — giving personal attention, coaching, advising.

**Paragraph 3: Complementary Nature (互补性质)**

Transformational leadership **builds on** transactional leadership. They are **complementary, not opposing**. The best leaders are both transformational and transactional. Transformational leadership produces effort and performance **beyond** what transactional leadership alone can do. However, if a leader is only transactional without transformational qualities, they will be mediocre. The **full range of leadership model** shows that the four I's result in extra effort, higher productivity, higher morale and satisfaction, lower turnover and absenteeism, and greater organizational adaptability.

**Paragraph 4: Evidence of Effectiveness (有效性证据)**

Research indicates: (a) Transformational leadership is more strongly correlated with **lower turnover**, **higher productivity**, **lower stress and burnout**, and **higher satisfaction**. (b) It works across many contexts — business, military, education, government, sports. (c) **Contingent reward** (a transactional behavior) sometimes works as well as transformational leadership. (d) Transformational leadership can be developed — it is not entirely innate.

**Paragraph 5: Practical Implications (实践启示)**

Organizations should: (a) Develop transformational leadership through training in vision articulation, charisma, and individualized consideration. (b) Use transformational leadership during times of change and crisis. (c) Maintain transactional leadership for routine operations and compliance. (d) Select leaders who demonstrate both transformational and transactional qualities. (e) Build trust through integrity, benevolence, and ability — the three foundations of trust that support transformational leadership.

**Conclusion**: Transformational and transactional leadership are complementary. The most effective leaders use both, with transformational leadership producing extra effort and performance beyond what transactional leadership alone achieves.

</details>

### 题目8：权力基础与权力策略 / Question 8: Power Bases and Power Tactics (35分)

**题目 / Question**:

Discuss the five bases of power and explain why personal power is more effective than formal power. What power tactics are most effective, and how should managers use power ethically?

讨论五种权力基础，解释为什么个人权力比正式权力更有效。哪些权力策略最有效，管理者如何道德地使用权力？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Power (定义权力)**

**Power** (权力) is the capacity that A has to influence the behavior of B so B acts in accordance with A's wishes. Power is a capacity or potential — it may exist but not be exercised. Power requires **dependence** (依赖性) — the greater B's dependence on A, the greater A's power.

**Paragraph 2: Five Bases of Power (五种权力基础)**

**Formal power** (正式权力): (a) **Coercive power** (强制权力) — based on fear of negative results; (b) **Reward power** (奖赏权力) — based on ability to distribute rewards; (c) **Legitimate power** (法定权力) — based on position in formal hierarchy. **Personal power** (个人权力): (d) **Expert power** (专家权力) — based on expertise, special skills, or knowledge; (e) **Referent power** (参照权力) — based on identification, admiration, and respect.

**Paragraph 3: Why Personal Power is More Effective (为什么个人权力更有效)**

Research shows: (a) **Expert and referent power** are positively related to employee satisfaction, commitment, and performance. (b) **Reward and legitimate power** are unrelated to these outcomes. (c) **Coercive power** often generates resentment and resistance. Personal power creates **genuine commitment** rather than mere **compliance**. It builds trust and respect, while formal power may create resentment or mere obedience. Personal power is also more sustainable — it cannot be taken away by organizational changes.

**Paragraph 4: Effective Power Tactics (有效的权力策略)**

Nine power tactics ranked from most to least effective: (a) **Rational persuasion** (理性说服) — most effective across all levels; (b) **Inspirational appeals** (情感诉求); (c) **Consultation** (咨询); (d) **Ingratiating tactics** (讨好策略); (e) **Personal appeals** (个人诉求); (f) **Exchange** (交换); (g) **Coalition tactics** (联盟策略); (h) **Pressure** (压力); (i) **Legitimating tactics** (合法化策略) — least effective. Managers should rely on rational persuasion and inspirational appeals rather than pressure and coercion.

**Paragraph 5: Ethical Use of Power (道德使用权力)**

Managers should use power ethically by: (a) Using power for organizational goals, not personal gain. (b) Being transparent about the basis of power. (c) Avoiding manipulation and coercion. (d) Building expertise and relationships (personal power) rather than relying on position. (e) Recognizing that power comes with responsibility. (f) Avoiding **defensive behaviors** (防御性行为) like buck passing, playing safe, and resisting change. (g) Using **impression management** (印象管理) ethically — genuine self-presentation rather than manipulation.

**Conclusion**: Personal power (expert and referent) is more effective than formal power. Managers should develop personal power through expertise and relationship building, use rational persuasion as their primary tactic, and exercise power ethically.

</details>

### 题目9：组织变革阻力与克服 / Question 9: Resistance to Change and Overcoming It (30分)

**题目 / Question**:

Discuss the individual and organizational sources of resistance to change. What tactics can managers use to overcome resistance, and which are most effective?

讨论个体和组织对变革阻力的来源。管理者可以使用哪些策略来克服阻力，哪些最有效？

<details>
<summary>📝 参考答案框架 / Answer Framework</summary>

**Paragraph 1: Define Change and Resistance (定义变革和阻力)**

**Change** (变革) is making things different. **Planned change** (计划变革) is intentional, goal-oriented activity. Resistance to change is a natural human and organizational response. Understanding its sources is essential for effective change management.

**Paragraph 2: Individual Sources of Resistance (个体阻力来源)**

Five individual sources: (a) **Habit** (习惯) — programmed responses; when faced with change, habitual responses are convenient. (b) **Security** (安全感) — change threatens safety; high need for security resists change. (c) **Economic factors** (经济因素) — fear that change will lower productivity and reduce income. (d) **Fear of the unknown** (未知恐惧) — change replaces ambiguity and uncertainty; employees resist because they don't know the consequences. (e) **Selective information processing** (选择性信息处理) — individuals hear what they want to hear; they resist change that challenges their worldviews.

**Paragraph 3: Organizational Sources of Resistance (组织阻力来源)**

Five organizational sources: (a) **Structural inertia** (结构惯性) — built-in stability mechanisms (selection process, performance evaluation, formalization) resist change. (b) **Limited focus of change** (有限焦点) — organizations are interdependent subsystems; changing one part affects others. (c) **Group inertia** (群体惯性) — group norms act as constraints on individual change efforts. (d) **Threat to expertise** (专长威胁) — changes in technology or structure threaten the expertise of established groups. (e) **Threat to established power relationships** (权力关系威胁) — redistribution of decision-making authority threatens established power balances.

**Paragraph 4: Eight Tactics to Overcome Resistance (八种克服阻力策略)**

Ranked from most to least effective: (a) **Education and communication** (教育和沟通) — most effective when resistance stems from misinformation. (b) **Participation** (参与) — effective when resistors have expertise to contribute. (c) **Building support and commitment** (建立支持和承诺) — training, counseling, therapy help employees cope. (d) **Developing positive relationships** (发展积极关系) — trust and support reduce resistance. (e) **Fair implementation** (公平实施) — when people perceive process as fair, they accept outcomes more readily. (f) **Manipulation and cooptation** (操纵和拉拢) — covert tactics; can backfire when discovered. (g) **Selecting people who accept change** (选择接受变革的人) — proactive strategy. (h) **Coercion** (强制) — least effective; last resort; generates resentment.

**Paragraph 5: Best Practices (最佳实践)**

Best practice is to begin with **softer tactics** (communication, participation, support) and move to harder tactics only if necessary. Managers should: (a) Create a sense of urgency (Kotter's first step). (b) Build a guiding coalition. (c) Communicate the vision clearly. (d) Empower employees to act. (e) Generate short-term wins to build momentum. (f) Consolidate gains and anchor new approaches in culture.

**Conclusion**: Resistance to change is inevitable but manageable. Understanding its sources and using appropriate tactics can significantly reduce resistance and increase change success.
