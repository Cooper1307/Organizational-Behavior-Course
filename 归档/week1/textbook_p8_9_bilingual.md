## Contingency Variables (权变变量 - 续)

...person's behavior changes in different situations. For instance, not everyone is motivated by money, and people may behave differently at a religious service than they do at a party.
个人的行为在不同情境下会发生变化。例如，并非每个人都受金钱激励，人们在宗教仪式上的表现可能与在派对上截然不同。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Motivated by money**: 受金钱激励
* **Religious service**: 宗教仪式

This doesn't mean, of course, that we can't offer reasonably accurate explanations of human behavior or make valid predictions.
当然，这并不意味着我们无法对人类行为提供合理的准确解释或做出有效的预测。

It does mean that OB concepts must reflect situational, or contingency, conditions.
它意味着组织行为学（OB）的概念必须反映情境或权变条件。

We can say x leads to y, but only under conditions specified in z—the contingency variables.
我们可以说 x 导致 y，但仅限于在 z 指定的条件下——即权变变量。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Specified**: 指定/指定的
* **Contingency variables**: 权变变量/情境变量

The science of OB was developed by applying general concepts to a particular situation, person, or group.
组织行为学这门科学是通过将一般概念应用于特定情境、个人或群体而发展起来的。

For example, OB scholars would avoid stating that everyone likes complex and challenging work (a general concept). Why? Because not everyone wants a challenging job.
例如，OB 学者会避免声称每个人都喜欢复杂且具有挑战性的工作（这是一个一般概念）。为什么？因为并非每个人都想要一份具有挑战性的工作。

Some people prefer routine over varied work, or simple over complex tasks. A job attractive to one person may be unattractive to another; its appeal is contingent on the person who holds it.
有些人更喜欢常规工作而非多变的工作，或者喜欢简单的任务而非复杂的任务。对一个人有吸引力的工作可能对另一个人没有吸引力；其吸引力取决于持有该职位的人。

Often, we find both general effects (money does have some ability to motivate most of us) and contingencies (some of us are more motivated by money than others, and some situations are more about money than others).
通常，我们会发现既存在一般效应（金钱确实能激励我们中的大多数人），也存在权变情况（我们中有些人受金钱激励的程度高于他人，而有些情境与金钱的关系比其他情境更紧密）。

We best understand OB when we realize how both (general effects and the contingencies that affect them) often guide behavior.
当我们意识到这二者（一般效应以及影响它们的权变因素）通常共同指引行为时，我们才能最好地理解 OB。

## CHALLENGES AND OPPORTUNITIES FOR OB (组织行为学的挑战与机遇)

Understanding organizational behavior has never been more important for managers.
对管理者来说，理解组织行为从未像现在这样重要。

Take a quick look at the dramatic changes in organizations. The typical employee is getting older; the workforce is becoming increasingly diverse; and global competition requires employees to become more flexible and cope with rapid change.
快速浏览一下组织中发生的巨大变化：典型的员工正在变老；劳动力变得日益多元化；全球竞争要求员工变得更加灵活并应对快速变化。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Dramatic changes**: 剧变
* **Increasingly diverse**: 日益多元化
* **Flexible**: 灵活的
* **Cope with**: 应对/应付

As a result of these changes and others, employment options have adapted to include new opportunities for workers. Exhibit 1-2 details some of the types of options individuals may find offered to them by organizations or for which they would like to negotiate.
由于这些变化及其他因素，就业选择已经调整，为工作者包含了新的机遇。图表 1-2 详细列出了一些个人可能会发现组织向其提供的、或者他们希望协商的就业选择类型。

Under each heading in the exhibit, you will find a grouping of options from which to choose—or combine.
在图表中的每个标题下，你会发现一组可供选择或组合的选择。

For instance, at one point in your career you may find yourself employed full time in an office in a localized, nonunion setting with a salary and bonus compensation package, while at another point you may wish to negotiate for a flextime, virtual position and choose to work from overseas for a combination of salary and extra paid time off.
例如，在你职业生涯的某个阶段，你可能会发现自己在一个局部的、非工会环境下全职办公，并享有薪水加奖金的薪酬组合；而在另一个阶段，你可能希望协商一种弹性工作时间、虚拟职位，并选择在海外工作，享受薪水和额外带薪休假的组合。

In short, today's challenges bring opportunities for managers to use OB concepts. In this section, we review some—but not nearly all—of the critical developing issues confronting managers for which OB offers solutions or, at least, meaningful insights toward solutions.
简而言之，当今的挑战为管理者提供了使用 OB 概念的机遇。在本节中，我们将回顾一些（但远非全部）管理者面临的关键发展中的问题，OB 为此提供了解决方案，或者至少是能够导向解决方案的有意义的见解。

## Continuing Globalization (持续的全球化)

Organizations are no longer constrained by national borders. Samsung, the largest South Korean business conglomerate, sells most of its products to organizations in other countries; Burger King is owned by a Brazilian firm; and McDonald’s sells hamburgers in 118 countries on 6 continents.
组织不再受国界的限制。韩国最大的商业集团三星将其大部分产品销往其他国家的组织；汉堡王由一家巴西公司持有；麦当劳在 6 大洲的 118 个国家销售汉堡。

Even Apple—arguably the U.S. company with the strongest U.S. identity—employs twice as many workers outside the United States as it does inside the country.
即使是苹果公司——这可以说是美国特色最强的美国公司——在海外雇佣的员工数量也是国内的两倍。

And all major automobile makers now manufacture cars outside their borders; Honda builds cars in Ohio, Ford in Brazil, Volkswagen in Mexico, and both Mercedes and BMW in the United States and South Africa.
所有主要的汽车制造商现在都在国界之外制造汽车；本田在俄亥俄州建厂，福特在巴西，大众在墨西哥，梅赛德斯和宝马在美国和南非都有工厂。

The world has become a global village. In the process, the manager's job has changed. Effective managers anticipate and adapt their approaches to the global issues we discuss next.
世界已变成一个全球村。在这个过程中，管理者的工作发生了变化。有效的管理者会预见并调整他们的方法，以应对我们接下来讨论的全球性问题。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Global village**: 地球村
* **Anticipate**: 预见
* **Adapt their approaches**: 调整他们的方法
