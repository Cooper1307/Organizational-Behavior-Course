## WORKING WITH PEOPLE FROM DIFFERENT CULTURES (与不同文化背景的人共事)

In your own country or on foreign assignment, you'll find yourself working with bosses, peers, and other employees born and raised in different cultures.
无论是在你自己的国家还是在国外工作，你都会发现自己正在与出生并成长于不同文化背景的上司、同事及其他员工共同工作。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Foreign assignment**: 外派任务/驻外工作
* **Peers**: 同事/同级

What motivates you may not motivate them. Or your communication style may be straightforward and open, which others may find uncomfortable and threatening.
能够激励你的因素可能无法激励他们。或者，你的沟通方式可能是直接且公开的，而其他人可能会觉得这种方式令人不适且具有威胁性。

To work effectively with people from different cultures, you need to understand how their culture and background have shaped them and how to adapt your management style to fit any differences.
为了与来自不同文化背景的人有效合作，你需要了解他们的文化 and 背景是如何塑造他们的，并学会如何调整你的管理风格以适应这些差异。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Shaped them**: 塑造了他们
* **Adapt your management style**: 调整你的管理风格

## ADAPTING TO DIFFERING CULTURAL AND REGULATORY NORMS (适应不同的文化和监管准则)

To be effective, managers need to know the cultural norms of the workforce in each country where they do business. For instance, in some countries a large percentage of the workforce enjoys long holidays.
为了提高效率，管理者需要了解他们开展业务的每个国家的劳动力文化准则。例如，在某些国家，很大比例的劳动力享有长假。

There are national and local regulations to consider, too. Managers of subsidiaries abroad need to be aware of the unique financial and legal regulations applying to “guest companies” or else risk violating them.
此外，还需要考虑国家和地方的法规。海外子公司的管理者需要意识到适用于“客方公司”的独特财务和法律规定，否则可能面临违规风险。

Violations can have implications for their operations in that country and also for political relations between countries.
违规行为可能会影响他们在该国的业务运作，甚至影响国家间的政治关系。

Managers also need to be cognizant of differences in regulations for competitors in that country; many times, understanding the laws can lead to success or failure.
管理者还需要认识到该国竞争对手的法规差异；很多时候，对法律的理解可能决定成败。

For example, knowing local banking laws allowed one multinational firm to seize control of a storied (and very valuable) London building, Grosvenor House, from under the nose of the owner, the Indian hotel group Sahara.
例如，由于了解当地银行法，一家跨国公司成功地从所有者——印度撒哈拉酒店集团的眼皮底下，夺取了伦敦一栋具有历史意义（且非常昂贵）的建筑，即格罗夫纳屋酒店的控制权。

Management at Sahara contended that the loan default that led to the seizure was a misunderstanding regarding one of their other properties in New York. Globalization can get complicated.
撒哈拉集团管理层则辩称，导致没收的贷款违约是对其在纽约另一处房产的误解。全球化可能会变得非常复杂。

## Workforce Demographics (劳动力人口特征)

The workforce has always adapted to variations in the economy, longevity, birth rates, socioeconomic conditions, and other changes that have a widespread impact.
劳动力始终在适应经济波动、寿命、出生率、社会经济状况以及其他具有广泛影响的变化。

People adapt to survive, and OB studies the way those adaptations affect individuals' behavior.
人们为了生存而进行调适，而组织行为学（OB）研究这些调适如何影响个人行为。

For instance, even though the 2008 global recession ended years ago, some trends from those years are continuing: many people who have been long unemployed have left the workforce, while others have cobbled together several part-time jobs or settled for on-demand work.
例如，尽管 2008 年全球经济衰退早已结束，但当时的一些趋势仍在延续：许多长期失业的人已经离开了劳动力队伍，而另一些人则拼凑了几份兼职工作，或者满足于按需分配的工作。

Further options that have been particularly popular for younger educated workers have included obtaining specialized industry training after college, accepting full-time jobs that are lower-level, and starting their own companies.
对受过教育的年轻工作者特别受欢迎的进一步选择包括：大学毕业后接受专门的行业培训、接受职级较低的全职工作，以及创办自己的公司。

As students of OB, we can investigate what factors lead employees to make various choices and how their experiences affect their perceptions of their workplaces. In turn, this can help us predict organizational outcomes.
作为 OB 的学生，我们可以调查哪些因素导致员工做出各种选择，以及他们的经历如何影响他们对工作场所的看法。反过来，这可以帮助我们预测组织结果。

## Workforce Diversity (劳动力多元化)

One of the most important challenges for organizations is workforce diversity, a trend by which organizations are becoming more heterogeneous in terms of employees' gender, age, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, and other characteristics.
组织面临的最重要挑战之一是劳动力多元化，即组织在员工性别、年龄、种族、民族、性取向和其他特征方面变得更加异质化的趋势。

**术语对照 (Terminology):**

* **Workforce diversity**: 劳动力多元化
* **Heterogeneous**: 异质化的/多样的
* **Sexual orientation**: 性取向
* **Characteristics**: 特征/特点

Managing this diversity is a global concern. Though we have more to say about it in the next chapter, suffice it to say here that diversity presents great opportunities and poses challenging questions for managers and employees.
管理这种多元化是一个全球性的关注点。尽管我们将在下一章详细讨论，但在这里只需说明，多元化为管理者和员工带来了巨大的机遇，也提出了具有挑战性的问题。

How can we leverage differences within groups for competitive advantage? Should we treat all employees alike? Should we recognize individual and cultural differences? What are the legal requirements in each country? Does increasing diversity even matter?
我们如何利用群体内的差异来获得竞争优势？我们是否应该一视同仁地对待所有员工？我们是否应该承认个人和文化差异？各国的法定要求是什么？增加多元化真的重要吗？

## Social Media (社交媒体)

As we discuss in Chapter 9, social media in the business world is here to stay. Despite its pervasiveness, many organizations continue to struggle with employees' use of social media in the workplace.
正如我们在第 9 章讨论的那样，商业世界中的社交媒体将长期存在。尽管它无处不在，但许多组织仍在为员工在工作场所使用社交媒体而挣扎。

For instance, in February 2015, a Texas pizzeria fired an employee before her first day of work because she tweeted unflattering comments about her future job.
例如，2015 年 2 月，德克萨斯州的一家比萨店在一名员工入职第一天前就将其解雇，因为她在推特上发布了关于未来工作的负面评论。
